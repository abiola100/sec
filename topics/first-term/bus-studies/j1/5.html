COMMERCE
Meaning of Commerce

Commerce is the part of business that deals with trade and activities that help trade.

  • It involves buying and selling goods and services to satisfy human wants.

  • Example: A shop selling rice and sugar, or a bank providing money services.




Importance of Commerce
  1. Economic growth: Helps the country develop.

  2. Employment: Provides jobs for workers in shops, banks, transport, etc.

  3. Improved standard of living: Makes goods and services available.

  4. Government revenue: Taxes on goods, services, and trade provide money for the government.

  5. Promotes trade relations: Helps countries cooperate through import and export.

  6. Example: Importing oil from another country or exporting cocoa.



Activities that Aid Commerce

These are the Activities that make trade easier and faster:

  1. Transport: Moving goods from one place to another.
    • Example: Trucks delivering goods from a factory to a shop.

  2. Banking: Providing financial services like savings, loans, and payments.
    • Example: Banks giving loans to traders.

  3. Insurance: Protecting goods, businesses, and workers from risks.
    • Example: Insuring goods against fire or theft.

  4. Advertising: Informing people about products to encourage buying.
    • Example: Posters, radio ads, or social media ads.

  5. Warehousing: Storing goods safely until needed.
    • Example: Rice stored in a warehouse before being sold.




Division of Commerce

Commerce is divided into Home Trade and Foreign Trade.

  1. Home Trade (Domestic Trade) : These are Trade that takes place within the country.

    2. Types of Home Trade:

    1. Wholesale Trade : Buying goods in large quantities from producers and selling to retailers.

      • Example: A company buying 1000 bags of rice and selling to smaller shops.

    2. Retail Trade : Selling goods directly to consumers in small quantities.

      • Example: A shop selling rice and sugar to individual customers.


  2. Foreign Trade (International Trade) : These are Trade that takes place between countries.

    3. Types of Foreign Trade:

    1. Import: Buying goods from other countries.

      • Example: Nigeria importing vehicles from Japan.

    2. Export: Selling goods to other countries.

      • Example: Nigeria exporting cocoa to the United Kingdom.
