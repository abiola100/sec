COMMERCE
Meaning of Commerce

Commerce refers to all the activities that take place in the distribution and exchange of goods and services.

Commerce connects the producer to the final consumer by making goods available at the right place and at the right time.

Commerce includes both trade and aids to trade.



DIVISION OF COMMERCE

Commerce is divided into two major parts:

  • Trade

  • Aids to trade


    •

  1. TRADE

    2. Trade refers to the buying and selling of goods and services.

    Trade is divided into:

    A. Home Trade

    This refers to trade carried out within a country.

    It has two branches:

    1. Wholesale Trade

    2. Retail Trade

    B. Foreign Trade

    This refers to trade carried out between different countries.

    It has three branches:

    1. Import Trade – buying goods from another country

    2. Export Trade – selling goods to another country

    3. Entrepôt Trade – importing goods and re-exporting them

  2. AIDS TO TRADE

    3. Aids to trade refer to services that make trade easy and faster. They support movement, financing, storage, promotion, and communication of goods.

    The aids to trade are:

    a. Transportation

    The movement of goods and people. Examples: road, rail, air, water, pipeline.

    b. Banking

    Banks provide services such as saving money, lending money, advice, and safe keeping.

    c. Insurance

    Insurance provides protection against loss, damage, or accidents.

    d. Communication

    The process of sending and receiving information.

    e. Warehousing

    The storage of goods until they are needed.

    f. Advertising

    Advertising informs, persuades, and reminds consumers about goods.



IMPORTANCE OF COMMERCE
  1. Commerce helps to improve the movement of goods.

  2. Commerce provides employment opportunities.

  3. Commerce helps to improve the standard of living.

  4. Commerce promotes international relationships.

  5. Commerce supports industrial development.

  6. Commerce improves business security through insurance.

  7. Commerce improves communication between buyers and sellers.


TRADE
Wholesale Trade

A wholesaler buys in large quantities and sells in smaller quantities.

Functions of Wholesalers:

  1. They help producers by buying in bulk.

  2. They help retailers by selling smaller units.

  3. They help to improve storage of goods.

  4. They help to improve transportation.

  5. They help to improve finance by giving credit.

  6. They provide market information.

Retail Trade

A retailer sells goods in small quantities to consumers.

Types of Retailers:

  1. Hawkers and pedlars

  2. Roadside sellers

  3. Market traders

  4. Kiosk owners

  5. Supermarkets

  6. Departmental stores

  7. Chain stores

  8. Mail order businesses

Functions of Retailers:

  1. They sell goods in small units.

  2. They give credit to customers.

  3. They display goods.

  4. They provide after-sales service.

  5. They give information to consumers.

  6. They pass consumer feedback to producers.


FOREIGN TRADE

Foreign trade is the exchange of goods and services across national boundaries.

Types of Foreign Trade:

  1. Import

  2. Export

  3. Entrepôt

Problems of Foreign Trade:

  1. Language differences

  2. Tariffs and import duties

  3. Distance

  4. Poor transportation

  5. Political problems

  6. Exchange rate differences


BUSINESS DOCUMENTS USED IN COMMERCE
  1. Invoice – shows details of goods sold and the amount due

  2. Receipt – proof of payment

  3. Quotation – shows cost before buying

  4. Order form – used to request goods

  5. Delivery note – shows goods have been delivered

  6. Debit note – informs buyer of more money owed

  7. Credit note – informs buyer of money deducted


DIFFERENCE BETWEEN COMMERCE AND ECONOMICS

Economics deals with managing scarce resources.

Commerce deals with the exchange and distribution of goods and services.



FACTORS THAT AFFECT COMMERCE
  1. Level of technology

  2. Government policies

  3. Level of income

  4. Availability of transport

  5. Population size

  6. Availability of markets

  7. Political stability
