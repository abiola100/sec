DIVITION OF COMMERCE



Commerce can be divided into several key areas, each focusing on different aspects of the buying and selling of goods and services. Here are the main divisions:

(1) Trade (2) Aids to Trade
(3) E-commerce (4) Banking and Financial Services
(5) Auxiliary Services:



TRADE

Trade is divided into two:

Domestic/Home Trade International/Foreign Trade
  1. Domestic Trade: Buying and selling goods within the same country.

    • Wholesale Trade: Buying goods in large quantities from producers and selling them in smaller quantities to retailers.

    • Retail Trade: Selling goods in small quantities directly to consumers.

  2. International Trade: Buying and selling goods between countries.

    • Export Trade: Selling goods to other countries.

    • Import Trade: Buying goods from other countries.

    • Entrepot Trade: Importing goods for the purpose of re-exporting them.


Aids to Trade

  1. Transport: Moving goods from one place to another.

  2. Warehousing: Storing goods until they are needed.

  3. Banking and Finance: Providing financial services like loans, payments, and investments.

  4. Insurance: Protecting businesses against risks and losses.

  5. Advertising and Marketing: Promoting products and services to attract customers.

  6. Communication: Facilitating the exchange of information between businesses and consumers.


E-commerce:
  1. Online Retailing: Selling goods directly to consumers through websites and apps.

  2. Business-to-Business (B2B): Transactions between businesses conducted online.

  3. Business-to-Consumer (B2C): Transactions between businesses and individual consumers conducted online.

  4. Consumer-to-Consumer (C2C) : Transactions between individual consumers, often through online platforms.


Banking and Financial Services:
  • Commercial Banks: Provide everyday banking services such as deposits, loans, and payments.

  • Investment Banks: Assist in raising capital, mergers, and acquisitions.

  • Insurance Companies: Offer protection against various risks.

  • Stock Markets: Platforms for buying and selling stocks and other securities.


Auxiliary Services:
  • Legal Services: Ensuring businesses comply with laws and regulations.

  • Accounting and Bookkeeping: Managing financial records and ensuring accurate financial reporting.

  • Consulting Services: Providing expert advice to improve business operations.

  • Logistics Services: Managing the flow of goods from origin to destination efficiently.
