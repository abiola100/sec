OFFICE EQUIPMENT




Office equipment can be defined as the tools and devices used in a workplace to facilitate daily operations and activities.


TYPES AND USES OF OFFICE EQUIPMENTS


  1. Computers : Electronic devices for processing information, usually comprising a monitor, keyboard, and mouse.

  2. Printers: Machines that produce hard copies of documents and images from digital files. They can be inkjet or laser types.

  3. Copiers: Equipment that makes duplicate copies of documents, often with additional features like scanning and faxing.

  4. Telephones: Devices used for voice communication, including traditional landlines and mobile phones.

  5. Fax Machines: Machines that send and receive documents via telephone lines, scanning documents for transmission.

  6. Scanners: Devices that convert physical documents into digital format for storage or sharing.

  7. Projectors: Equipment used to display visual content on a larger screen, commonly used for presentations.

  8. Office Furniture: Includes desks, chairs, filing cabinets, and shelving units that create a functional workspace.

  9. Shredders: Machines that cut paper into small pieces for destroying sensitive documents.

  10. Whiteboards/Blackboards: Surfaces used for writing and displaying information in meetings or presentations.



CARE FOR OFFICE EQUIPMENTS


  1. Computers: Clean screens and keyboards regularly.

  2. Printers: Clean print heads and use correct ink.

  3. Copiers: Keep glass clean and maintained.

  4. Telephones: Wipe handsets and avoid tangled cords.

  5. Fax Machines: Change ink regularly and dust often.

  6. Scanners: Clean glass and update software frequently.

  7. Projectors: Clean lens and store safely.

  8. Shredders: Oil blades and avoid overloading.

  9. Whiteboards/Chalkboards: Clean surfaces to prevent ghosting.

  10. Office Furniture: Check for loose screws and clean.

  11. Binding Machines: Store dust-free and follow instructions.

  12. Label Makers: Keep clean and replace cartridges.
