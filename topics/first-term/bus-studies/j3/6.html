COMMUNICATION
Meaning of Communication

Communication is the process of exchanging information, ideas, messages, or instructions between people or organizations to achieve understanding. In business, effective communication ensures smooth operations and coordination.

Objectives of Communication
  1. To share information and instructions accurately.

  2. To create understanding among employees, management, and customers.

  3. To facilitate decision-making in business.

  4. To maintain good relationships within and outside the organization.

  5. To persuade and influence people positively in business operations.


Importance of Communication in Business
  1. Facilitates Decision Making: Proper communication provides necessary information for making informed decisions.

  2. Enhances Efficiency: Ensures tasks are carried out correctly and on time.

  3. Promotes Teamwork: Employees understand their roles and cooperate effectively.

  4. Improves Customer Relations: Enables businesses to respond to customer needs and complaints.

  5. Reduces Conflicts: Clear instructions prevent misunderstandings and disputes.

  6. Supports Business Growth: Helps in marketing, sales, and expansion through proper dissemination of information.


Types of Communication

1. Oral Communication

Involves spoken words or verbal messages.

Examples: Meetings, discussions, telephone calls, presentations.

Advantages: Immediate feedback, quick, allows clarification.

Disadvantages: Messages may be forgotten, misheard, or misunderstood.

2. Written Communication

Involves messages recorded on paper or electronic media.

Examples: Letters, emails, memos, reports, notices.

Advantages: Permanent record, clear, can reach many people.

Disadvantages: Takes time to prepare and send, less personal interaction.

3. Visual Communication

Involves conveying messages using visual aids.

Examples: Charts, diagrams, posters, videos, signs.

Advantages: Easy to understand, attracts attention, simplifies complex ideas.

Disadvantages: Can be expensive to produce, limited in detail.

4. Non-Verbal Communication

Involves gestures, facial expressions, body language, and tone of voice.

Examples: Nodding, shaking hands, waving, posture, eye contact.

Advantages: Conveys emotions and attitudes, supports verbal messages.

Disadvantages: Can be misinterpreted, not always reliable alone.



Channels of Communication

Formal Communication: Follows the official hierarchy of the organization.

Examples: Reports, official letters, circulars, memos.

Informal Communication: Does not follow official channels; often called “grapevine.”

Examples: Conversations among colleagues, social interactions.



Barriers to Effective Communication
  1. Physical Barriers: Noise, distance, or faulty equipment that prevent message delivery.

  2. Language Barriers: Use of unfamiliar words, jargon, or foreign languages.

  3. Psychological Barriers: Stress, emotions, or attitude affecting understanding.

  4. Cultural Barriers: Differences in beliefs, customs, or traditions.

  5. Organizational Barriers: Poor structure, unclear channels, or hierarchy issues.


Principles of Effective Communication
  1. Clarity: Use simple and understandable language.

  2. Conciseness: Keep the message brief and to the point.

  3. Completeness: Provide all necessary information.

  4. Feedback: Ensure the receiver understands the message.

  5. Appropriateness: Match the message to the audience and situation.

  6. Timeliness: Communicate at the right time.


Services Provided by Communication Agencies

Communication agencies provide various services that enable the smooth exchange of information and messages between individuals, businesses, and organizations. These services are vital for both personal and business purposes.

1. Postal Services

Definition: The postal service involves sending letters, parcels, and documents from one place to another through a postal system.

Services Provided:

  1. Letter Delivery: Sending and receiving letters within the country and internationally.

  2. Parcel Service: Delivery of packages, goods, and documents.

  3. Express or Courier Services: Fast delivery of urgent letters and parcels.

  4. Registered Mail: Ensures security and proof of delivery.

  5. Post Office Savings: Some postal agencies provide financial services like savings accounts.

Importance:

  1. Reliable and secure way of sending documents and goods.

  2. Supports business correspondence.

  3. Provides evidence of communication through registered mail.

2. Telegraph Services

Definition: Telegraph services involve sending messages over long distances using electrical signals, usually in the form of coded text.

Services Provided:

  1. Telegrams: Sending urgent or important messages quickly.

  2. Official Communications: Often used by businesses or government organizations for fast message delivery.

Importance:

  1. Provides fast communication over long distances.

  2. Useful for urgent business transactions or notifications.

3. Telephone Services

Definition: Telecommunication services allow the exchange of information in real-time using voice, and sometimes data, through wired or mobile networks.

Services Provided:

  1. Voice Calls: Direct verbal communication with individuals or organizations.

  2. Conference Calls: Enables multiple parties to communicate simultaneously.

  3. Mobile Messaging (SMS/USSD): Sending short text messages for personal or business purposes.

  4. Internet/VoIP Services: Voice communication via the internet, supporting both calls and video chats.

Importance:

  1. Instant communication over long or short distances.

  2. Reduces delays in business decision-making.

  3. Enables personal and official communication efficiently.

4. Radio and Television Services

Definition: Broadcasting services allow messages, information, and advertisements to reach large audiences via radio waves or television signals.

Services Provided:

  1. Public Announcements: Informing the public about events, programs, or emergencies.

  2. Advertising: Businesses promote products and services to a wide audience.

  3. Education and Entertainment: Provides knowledge, news, and entertainment to the public.

Importance:

  1. Reaches a large number of people quickly.

  2. Useful for mass communication and advertising.

  3. Keeps the public informed about important events.

5. Internet and Email Services

Definition: Communication via the internet allows messages, documents, and media to be sent digitally over networks.

Services Provided:

  1. Email: Sending letters, reports, invoices, and official documents electronically.

  2. Social Media Communication: Connecting with customers and audiences online.

  3. Instant Messaging: Quick exchange of messages through platforms like WhatsApp, Telegram, or Slack.

Importance:

  1. Fast and cost-effective communication.

  2. Can reach local and international audiences instantly.

  3. Facilitates business operations, marketing, and customer service.

Summary:

Communication is vital in business as it enables the exchange of information, coordination, decision-making, and relationship building. It can be oral, written, visual, or non-verbal. Using appropriate channels and overcoming barriers ensures effective communication, which supports the success and growth of a business.
