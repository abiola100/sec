OFFICE PROCEDURE
Definition:

Office procedure refers to the systematic and organized methods used in performing tasks and carrying out activities in an office to ensure efficiency, accuracy, and proper documentation.



Importance of Office Procedure

Office procedures are important because :

  1. they Ensure that office activities are carried out systematically.

  2. they Reduce errors and confusion in office work.

  3. they Save time and improve efficiency.

  4. they Maintain proper records of business transactions.

  5. they Facilitate smooth communication within and outside the office.

  6. they Help in proper coordination of office work.

  7. they Promote accountability and responsibility among office staff.


Components of Office Procedure

Filing and Record Keeping

Keeping records of all transactions and correspondence systematically.

Methods include alphabetical, numerical, and subject filing.

Communication

Handling incoming and outgoing correspondence (letters, emails, memos).

Ensuring messages are passed accurately and timely.

Reception of Visitors

Greeting and attending to clients, guests, or customers.

Maintaining a visitor log or record.

Telephone Etiquette

Answering calls politely and professionally.

Taking accurate messages and transferring calls properly.

Mail Handling

Sorting, distributing, and dispatching letters efficiently.

Maintaining records of incoming and outgoing mail.

Office Equipment Use

Proper handling of office tools like typewriters, computers, fax machines, photocopiers, and printers.

Ensuring equipment is maintained and used correctly.

Diary and Appointment Management

Scheduling meetings, appointments, and official engagements.

Keeping reminders of important dates and deadlines.

Office Environment Management

Ensuring a clean, organized, and safe workplace.

Proper arrangement of furniture, files, and office equipment.



Principles of Good Office Procedure
  1. Simplicity: Procedures should be easy to understand and follow.

  2. Efficiency: Procedures should save time and resources.

  3. Accuracy: All records and communications should be correct.

  4. Standardization: Procedures should follow a standard format for consistency.

  5. Confidentiality: Sensitive information must be kept secure.

  6. Accountability: Staff should be responsible for their actions and tasks.


Steps in Office Procedure
  1. Receiving Work: Identify the task or document that requires action.

  2. Processing: Carry out the task efficiently using proper methods.

  3. Recording: Keep a record of actions taken for future reference.

  4. Dispatching: Send out documents, letters, or messages to the appropriate person.

  5. Filing: Store documents in the correct file for easy retrieval.


Types of Office Procedure
  1. Routine Procedure: Regular daily tasks like filing, typing letters, answering calls.

  2. Standard Procedure: Established methods for handling specific tasks.

  3. Special Procedure: Procedures for unusual or emergency situations.


Qualities of a Good Office Procedure
  1. It is well-planned and organized.

  2. It is understandable by all staff.

  3. It ensures prompt execution of tasks.

  4. It maintains accuracy and completeness of information.

  5. It promotes smooth workflow in the office.



Procedures for Preparing Bills, Invoices, and Receipts

a. Bills

Definition:

A bill is a document requesting payment for goods supplied or services rendered.

Procedure for Preparing a Bill:

  1. Identify the customer or client.

  2. List all goods or services provided, with quantities and prices.

  3. Calculate the total amount due, including any taxes if applicable.

  4. Record the date of issue.

  5. Prepare the bill in the standard format.

  6. Send the bill to the customer for payment.

b. Invoices

Definition:

An invoice is an official document issued to a buyer stating the goods or services supplied and the amount payable.

Procedure for Preparing an Invoice:

  1. Write the invoice number for easy tracking.

  2. Include seller and buyer details (name and address).

  3. List goods/services supplied with quantity, unit price, and total cost.

  4. Include terms of payment and due date.

  5. Calculate the total amount payable including any taxes.

  6. Issue the invoice to the customer.

c. Receipts

Definition:

A receipt is a document acknowledging that payment has been received.

Procedure for Preparing a Receipt:

  1. Identify the payer (name and address).

  2. Record the amount received in words and figures.

  3. Mention the purpose of payment (e.g., goods, services, rent).

  4. Include the date of payment.

  5. Assign a receipt number for record-keeping.

  6. Issue the receipt to the payer and keep a copy for office records.


Procedure for Making Payments

a. Cash Payment

  1. Identify the amount to be paid and the payee.

  2. Prepare a cash voucher detailing the purpose of payment.

  3. Obtain authorization from the appropriate officer.

  4. Pay the amount in cash to the recipient.

  5. Record the transaction in the cash book.

  6. Issue a receipt if necessary.

b. Bank Transfer

  1. Obtain bank account details of the recipient.

  2. Fill the transfer form with the amount, date, and purpose.

  3. Obtain approval from the authorized officer.

  4. Submit the transfer form to the bank.

  5. Record the transaction in the bank book.

  6. Confirm receipt with the payee.

c. Cheque Payment

  1. Prepare a cheque with the correct amount and payee details.

  2. Fill the cheque voucher with payment details.

  3. Obtain authorization from the appropriate officer.

  4. Sign the cheque.

  5. Issue the cheque to the payee.

  6. Record the transaction in the cheque register and cash/bank book.


Bank Procedures
  1. Deposit of money: Prepare deposit slip, submit cash or cheque to the bank, and record in cash/bank book.

  2. Withdrawal of money: Fill withdrawal slip, obtain authorization, and update records.

  3. Bank reconciliation: Regularly check the bank statement against office records to correct errors.


Imprest Account

Definition:

An imprest account is a fixed amount of money given to an employee to make small or routine payments on behalf of the organization.

a. Maintaining the Imprest Account:

  1. The employee is given a fixed sum of money.

  2. Payments are made from this account for small expenses (e.g., office supplies).

  3. Receipts must be collected for every payment.

  4. The total expenses are recorded in the imprest account ledger.

b. Restoration of Imprest Account:

  1. Calculate total expenditure from the imprest account.

  2. Submit receipts and records to the accountant.

  3. The organization reimburses the exact amount spent to restore the account to its original balance.

  4. Maintain proper records for accountability.
