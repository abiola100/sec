MEANING OF AGRICULTURE

Agriculture can be define as the cultivation of plants, rearing of animals for human consumption. Agriculture practice mainly about growing of crops or raising of animals,such as poultries, cattle, goat,sheep,pig, cattle etc.



IMPORTANCE OF AGRICULTURE TO THE SOCIETY

  1. Provition of raw materia :   raw material are major source of economic growth. for any economy to grow there has to be constant inflow of raw materia including Agriculture produce such as animal skin for bags and shoe leather, rubber tree for production of rubber materials such as ball, waterproof cloths, and shoes etc.

  2. Ensuring food security :   Agriculture provide food for human being sustainability. Nutrients in food provide the energy human bodies need to function.

  3. Job opportunity :   Agriculture reduce the rate of unemployment in the society, there are various opportunity in different area of agriculture, such as live stock rearing, crops farming and fish farming.

  4. Rural development :   money made from agriculture can be used to develop rural community, such as building houses, improve electricity, water supply etc.

  5. Feeding of animals :   Agriculture provide food for animals to feed on, such as cow, chiken, pig etc. thereby produce things that people can use such as : milk, eggs, or wool etc.

  6. Improve greener environment :   green environment is crucial in maintaining the balance of carbon dioxide and oxygen on the planet. and also Preserve the World's Natural Resources, and also Lead to a Healthier Life.

  7. Build strong economy: Agriculture serve a major role in in boosting economic system by boosting labor productivity, increasing agricultural surplus to accumulate capital, and increasing foreign exchange via exports.

TYPES OF AGRICULTURE PRACTICE IN LOCALTIES

INCLUDE :
  1. Pastoral farming
  2. Arable farming
  3. Mixed farming
  4. Taungya farming
  5. Fish farming
  6. Livestock farming
  7. Shiftin cultivation
  8. Land rotation/Bush fallowing
  9. Crop rotation
  1. Pastoral farming : Pastoral farming is a form of farming that is focused on the raising of animals that feed on grasses and legumes, such as sheep, cattle and goat.

  2. Arable farming: this is a form of farming that involve growing of crops. this form of agriculture does not involve rearing of animals or grow fruit and vegetables. exaple include wheat,barley, yam etc

  3. Mixed farming : mixed farming can be define as a form of farming that involve both cultivation of land and rearing of animals. a mixed farmer can plant rice and at the same time keep cattle, sheep, pigs or poultries.

  4. taungya farming : which combines the production of both arable and forest tree cops simultaneously on a piece of land.

  5. fish farmin : this is a form of farming or aquaculture in which fish are raised in enclosures to be sold as food. It is the fastest growing area of animal food production.

  6. Livestock farming : this is a form of farming that involve raising animals for business purpose. commodities such as meat, milk, egg,wool, and leather are produced by domesticated animals.

  7. Shiftin cultivation : this can be define as an agricultural system in which plots of land are cultivated temporarily, then abandoned while post-disturbance fallow vegetation is allowed to freely grow while the cultivator moves on to another plot.

  8. Crop rotation : Crop rotation is the practice of planting different crops sequentially on the same plot of land to improve soil health, optimize nutrients in the soil, and combat pest and weed pressure. for example a farmer may plant maize this year and plant cassava the following year
