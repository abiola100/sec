FARM STRUCTURE AND BUILDING
Meaning of farm structure and farm building

A farm structure is any building, shelter, enclosure, or facility constructed on a farm to support agricultural production and management activities.

A farm building is a constructed edifice designed to house people, animals, crops, tools, machines, or agricultural products.

Examples of farm structures include:

  1. Barns

  2. Silos

  3. Rhombus

  4. Fences

  5. Water supply facilities

  6. Drainage systems

  7. Processing sheds

  8. Farmhouses

Examples of farm buildings include:

  1. Poultry houses

  2. Cattle pens

  3. Pigsties

  4. Farm stores

  5. Offices

  6. Utility buildings

  7. Security posts


Importance of Farm Structures and Buildings
  1. They provide shelter for animals, farm workers, and equipment.

  2. They protect crops, tools, and produce from rain, sun, pests, and theft.

  3. They make farm operations more efficient and organized.

  4. They reduce post-harvest losses and wastage.

  5. They help to ensure good sanitation and safety.

  6. They make record-keeping and management easier.

  7. They enhance productivity and farm expansion.


Types of Farm Structures

Farm structures are broadly divided into two groups:

  • Farm Structures (for storage and protection)

  • Farm Buildings (for housing humans and animals)

(i) Farm Structures

1. Barn

Definition: A barn is a large farm structure used for storing hay, grains, or other harvested crops and sometimes for housing animals.

Uses:

  1. Storing harvested produce like maize, yam, and hay.

  2. Protecting produce from rain, sun, and pests.

  3. Keeping farm implements and animal feed.

Features:

  1. Good ventilation.

  2. Strong walls and roofing.

  3. Raised floor to prevent dampness.

2. Silo

Definition: A silo is a tall, tower-like structure used for storing and preserving large quantities of grains and silage (animal feed) under airtight conditions.

Types:

  1. Tower silo

  2. Pit silo

  3. Trench silo

Uses:

  1. Storage of maize, wheat, and other grains.

  2. Preservation of silage for livestock.

Features:

  1. Airtight container.

  2. Made of metal, concrete, or plastic.

  3. Located near animal houses for easy feed access.

3. Rhombus (or Rhombic House)

Definition: A rhombus is a local type of storage structure mainly used for storing grains like maize, millet, sorghum, and beans.

Features:

  1. Made of bamboo, mud, or thatch.

  2. Elevated above the ground using wooden poles.

  3. Has small ventilation holes.

Uses:

  1. Storing dry grains and seeds.

  2. Protecting crops from rodents and moisture.

4. Fences and Enclosures

Definition: These are barriers built around the farm or livestock area to prevent trespassing or movement of animals.

Types:

  1. Wire fence

  2. Wooden fence

  3. Live (hedge) fence

  4. Concrete fence

Uses:

  1. Protecting crops from animals.

  2. Keeping livestock within the farm.

  3. Defining farm boundaries.

5. Water and Drainage Structures

Examples: Wells, boreholes, tanks, irrigation channels, and drainage ditches.

Uses:

  1. Provide clean water for animals and crops.

  2. Remove excess water to prevent flooding.

(ii) Farm Buildings

1. Poultry House or Pen

Definition: A poultry house is a building used to rear domestic birds such as chickens, ducks, and turkeys.

Features:

  1. Good ventilation.

  2. Dry, clean, and well-drained floor.

  3. Nesting boxes and perches.

  4. Fencing to protect birds from predators.

Uses:

  1. Rearing poultry birds for eggs and meat.

  2. Protecting birds from sun, rain, and diseases.

2. Store or Farm Store

Definition: A store is a building used for keeping harvested crops, seeds, fertilizers, and farm tools.

Features:

  1. Rodent-proof and dry.

  2. Proper ventilation.

  3. Elevated floor to prevent dampness.

Uses:

  1. Storing produce, equipment, and chemicals.

  2. Preventing spoilage and theft.

3. Office

Definition: A farm office is a small building or room where administrative work, record-keeping, and farm planning take place.

Features:

  1. Desks, chairs, filing cabinets, and stationery.

  2. Safe for keeping farm records and money.

Uses:

  1. Farm management and planning.

  2. Storage of important documents and receipts.

4. Utility Building

Definition: A utility building houses important facilities and equipment that support farm operations.

Examples: Workshop, maintenance shed, machinery house, or generator room.

Uses:

  1. Servicing and repairing tools.

  2. Housing power and mechanical systems.

Features:

  1. Adequate space and lighting.

  2. Tools and spare parts organized properly.

5. Security Post

Definition: A security post is a small structure at the farm entrance used by guards to monitor and protect the farm.

Features:

  1. Built near the gate.

  2. Small and comfortable for security staff.

Uses:

  1. Preventing theft and trespassing.

  2. Checking visitors entering or leaving the farm.


Factors to Consider When Siting Farm Structures and Buildings
  1. Location on high ground to prevent flooding.

  2. Availability of water and electricity.

  3. Nearness to roads and markets.

  4. Adequate space for future expansion.

  5. Wind direction â€” animal houses should be downwind of human houses.

  6. Proper drainage and waste disposal.

  7. Accessibility for vehicles and workers.


Maintenance of Farm Structures and Buildings
  1. Clean buildings regularly.

  2. Repair broken doors, roofs, and fences immediately.

  3. Paint metal parts to prevent rust.

  4. Treat wood against termites.

  5. Keep storage areas dry and pest-free.

  6. Inspect and service tools and machinery often.


Importance of Proper Maintenance
  1. It helps you to Prolong the lifespan of buildings.

  2. It helps you to Reduce repair costs.

  3. It helps you to Ensure safety and hygiene.

  4. It helps you to Improve productivity and efficiency.


Safety and Sanitation Practices
  1. Provide fire extinguishers and first aid kits.

  2. Keep chemicals in labeled and locked stores.

  3. Dispose of animal waste properly.

  4. Wash animal pens regularly.

  5. Provide clean water for both workers and animals.


Summary

Farm structures and buildings are essential facilities that support agricultural production, improve efficiency, and protect both people and farm resources. Proper design, siting, and maintenance of these structures help to increase farm productivity, reduce losses, and ensure a safe and hygienic working environment.
