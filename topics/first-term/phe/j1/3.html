Athletics refers to sporting activities that involve running, jumping, and throwing. It is a sport that develops the body through natural movements.

Athletics is sometimes called track and field events because some activities take place on the track (running), while others take place on the field (jumping and throwing).



Divisions of Athletics

Athletics is divided into two main parts:


  1. Track events

  2. Field events



  1. Track events

    hese are events that take place on the running track.

    Examples of track events include:


    • 100 metres

    • 200 metres

    • 400 metres

    • 800 metres

    • 1500 metres

    • Hurdles

    • Relays (e.g. 4 Ã— 100 metres relay)



  2. Field events

    These are events that take place on the field.

    They are divided into two groups:


    • Jumping events:

      • Long jump

      • High jump

    • Throwing events:

      • Shot put

      • Discus

      • Javelin



Importance of Athletics


  1. It helps to keep the body fit and healthy.

  2. It helps to develop strength and endurance.

  3. It helps in building sportsmanship and team spirit.

  4. It provides recreation and relaxation.



Field Events: Basic Skills and Techniques


Field events are divided into jumping and throwing events. Each event has basic skills that athletes must learn to participate properly and safely.

    • Throw: The discus is released at shoulder height with a wrist flick.

    • Follow-through: The throwing arm moves in the direction of the throw.

Rules Governing Field Events

Each field event has rules that guide how athletes should perform the event safely, fairly, and according to standard competition guidelines.

Let us look at the rules for each event:



Rules for Long Jump


  • The athlete must take off from the take-off board without stepping beyond it.

  • The jump is measured from the take-off line to the nearest mark made in the sandpit by any part of the body.

  • The athlete must land in the sandpit

  • A jump is counted as a no jump (foul) if the athlete:

    • Steps beyond the take-off board

    • Falls back on landing and touches the ground behind the mark made by the feet



Rules for High Jump


  • The athlete must take off from one foot only.

  • The athlete must jump over the bar without knocking it down.

  • Three consecutive failures (whether at one height or combined heights) will disqualify the athlete.

  • The height of the bar is raised after each round of successful jumps.

  • The athlete may choose to pass a height.



Rules for Shot Put


  • The athlete must put (push) the shot from the shoulder using one hand only â€” no throwing is allowed.

  • The shot must land within the marked throwing sector.

  • The athlete must stay inside the circle until the shot lands, and must leave from the rear half of the circle.

  • If the athlete steps out of the circle before the shot lands, it is a foul throw.



Rules for Discus Throw


  • The athlete must throw the discus from within the circle.

  • The discus must land inside the marked sector lines.

  • The athlete must not step out of the circle before the discus lands.

  • The athlete must leave the circle from the rear half after the throw.



Rules for Javelin Throw


  • The javelin must be held at the grip and thrown over the shoulder or upper arm â€” no slinging or turning allowed.

  • The javelin must land tip first within the marked sector.

  • The athlete must not cross the foul line at the end of the runway during or after the throw.

  • The athlete must stay behind the foul line until the javelin lands.
