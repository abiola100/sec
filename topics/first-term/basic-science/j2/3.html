RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ORGANISM IN THE SAME HABITAT

 Organisms in the same habitat interact in various ways, forming complex relationships that are essential for the ecosystem's balance.







TYPES OF RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ORGANISM IN THE SAME HABITAT
  1. Predation : Lions hunt and eat antelopes

  2. Competition : Plants compete for sunlight in the rainforest.

  3. Mutualism : Bees pollinate flowers while collecting nectar.

  4. Commensalism : Birds build nests in trees.

  5. Parasitism : Ticks feed on the blood of mammals.

  6. Herbivory : Elephants eat grasses and leaves.

  7. Symbiosis : Mycorrhizal fungi help plant roots absorb nutrients.

  8. Detritivory : Earthworms decompose dead plant material.
