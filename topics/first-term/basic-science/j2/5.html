MEASUREMENT OF GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT CHANGES

MEASUREMENT OF GROWTH
  1. eight/Length : Measure increase in height or length over time.

  2. Weight/Mass : Track changes in weight or mass periodically.

  3. Growth Rate : Calculate the rate of size increase over a specified period.





STAGES IN HUMAN DEVELOPMENT
  1. Childhood (Birth to 12 years): Rapid growth, learning, and development.

  2. Adolescence (12 to 18 years): Puberty, identity formation, and increasing independence.

  3. Adulthood (18 years and older): Establishing careers, relationships, and aging.




CHARACTERISTICS OF HUMAN DEVELOPMENT
  1. Physical Development: Changes in body size, proportions, appearance, and motor skills.

  2. Cognitive Development: Growth in thinking, learning, problem-solving, and understanding.

  3. Social-Emotional Development: Formation of relationships, understanding and expressing emotions, and developing social skills.

  4. Language Development: Acquisition, comprehension, and use of language.

  5. Identity Formation: Developing a sense of self, including values, beliefs, and personal goals.

  6. Independence: Increasing ability to take care of oneself and make decisions.

  7. Adaptability : Learning to adjust to new situations and challenges.
