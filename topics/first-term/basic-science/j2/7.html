CHEMICAL

A "chemical" is any substance made up of matter, which includes anything you can touch or see that is made of atoms and molecules. Examples of chemicals are water, salt, and the oxygen we breathe. They can be natural or made by people and are found in everything around us, from the food we eat to the cleaning products we use.

examples
  1. Water (H₂O): The most common solvent, essential for all known forms of life.

  2. Sodium Chloride (NaCl): Table salt, used in cooking and food preservation.

  3. Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄): Used in manufacturing fertilizers, batteries, and in various industrial processes.

  4. Ammonia (NH₃): Used in fertilizers, cleaning products, and refrigeration.

  5. Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆): A simple sugar, important energy source in living organisms.

  6. Acetone (C₃H₆O): A solvent used in nail polish remover and industrial applications.

  7. Methane (CH₄): A simple hydrocarbon, used as a fuel and a starting material for chemical synthesis.
