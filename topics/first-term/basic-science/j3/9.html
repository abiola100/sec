OZONE LAYER

The ozone layer can be define as a part of the Earth's atmosphere that protects us from the Sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays.



Ozone layer depletion means this protective layer is getting thinner, mainly because of human-made chemicals.



location of ozone layer in the atmosphere

The ozone layer is located in the stratosphere, which is a layer of the Earth's atmosphere. Specifically, it is found about 10 to 30 kilometers (6 to 19 miles) above the Earth's surface.








EFFECT OF OZONE LAYER DEPLETION
  1. Increased Skin Cancer Rates: More UV radiation reaching the Earth increases skin cancer risk.

  2. Higher Incidence of Cataracts: Increased UV exposure leads to more cases of cataracts and other eye problems.

  3. Harm to Plants: UV radiation damages plant life, affecting growth and crop yields.

  4. Damage to Phytoplankton: UV radiation harms phytoplankton, disrupting marine food chains.

  5. Health Issues in Animals: Increased UV exposure causes health problems in wildlife.

  6. Degradation of Materials: UV radiation breaks down materials like plastics, wood, fabrics, and rubber, shortening their lifespan.
