DESERTIFICATION

Desertification refers to the process by which fertile land becomes increasingly arid, unproductive, and degraded.

  It typically occurs in dryland regions, where the land loses its ability to support vegetation and agriculture due to factors such as deforestation, overgrazing, improper agricultural practices, climate change, and natural disasters.









GEOGRAPHICAL AREA WITH DESERTIFICATION IN NIGERAI
  1. Borno: Located in the northeastern part of Nigeria, Borno experiences severe desertification, exacerbated by conflicts that have displaced populations and disrupted traditional land management practices.

  2. Yobe: Also in the northeastern region, Yobe faces significant desertification challenges, impacting agricultural productivity and water resources.

  3. Adamawa: This northeastern state suffers from land degradation due to overgrazing, deforestation, and climate change.

  4. Kano: Located in the north-central region, Kano's agricultural lands are affected by soil erosion and loss of vegetation cover.

  5. Katsina: Situated in the northwest, Katsina experiences desertification that affects both rural and urban areas, leading to economic and environmental challenges.

  6. Jigawa: This northwestern state faces issues related to land degradation and reduced agricultural productivity due to desertification.

  7. Sokoto: In the extreme northwest, Sokoto's drylands are vulnerable to desertification, impacting local communities and their livelihoods.

  8. Bauchi: Located in the northeastern part of Nigeria, Bauchi is affected by deforestation and overuse of land resources, contributing to desertification.
