SOIL EROSION




Soil erosion is the process by which the top layer of soil is removed or worn away by natural forces such as water, wind, or ice, or through human activities like agriculture and deforestation.







CAUSES OF SOIL EROSION


  1. Heavy rainfall, runoff, and flooding can wash away soil.

  2. Strong winds can blow loose soil away, especially in dry areas.

  3. Removing trees and vegetation leaves soil exposed and more prone to erosion.

  4. Tilling, overgrazing, and improper farming practices disturb the soil structure.

  5. Building activities disturb soil and increase erosion risks.

  6. Changes in weather patterns can lead to more extreme weather events, increasing erosion.

  7. Lack of soil conservation practices can exacerbate erosion.


CONTROL OF EROSION


  1. Plant grass, trees, and shrubs to anchor soil with roots.

  2. Create terraces and plow along the contour of slopes.

  3. Cover soil with materials like straw or wood chips to prevent runoff.

  4. Reduce soil disturbance during farming practices.

  5. < Build barriers to block wind and water flow.

  6. Use blankets, fences, and basins to trap sediment.

  7. Redirect water away from vulnerable areas.

  8. Teach about erosion prevention methods and their importance.
