GLOBAL WARMING


Global warming is the long-term increase in Earth's average temperature due to human activities, especially the release of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO2).




CAUSES OF GLOBAL WARMING


  1. Burning Fossil Fuels: Using coal, oil, and natural gas for energy and transportation releases CO2.

  2. Deforestation: Cutting down forests reduces the number of trees that can absorb CO2.

  3. Industrial Processes: Factories and industries emit greenhouse gases.

  4. Agriculture: Livestock produce methane, and fertilizers release nitrous oxide.

  5. Waste Management: Landfills release methane as waste decomposes.


EFFECT OF GLOBAL WARMING


  1. Rising Temperatures: Hotter weather and more frequent heatwaves.

  2. Melting Ice Caps and Glaciers: Ice in polar regions melts, raising sea levels.

  3. Rising Sea Levels: Higher sea levels cause coastal flooding and erosion.

  4. Extreme Weather: More severe storms, hurricanes, droughts, and heavy rain.

  5. Impact on Wildlife: Habitats are disrupted, leading to loss of species.

  6. Agricultural Problems: Changing weather affects crop growth and food supply.





MITIGATION AND ADAPTATION TO GLOBAL WARMING


  1. Renewable Energy: Using solar, wind, and other clean energy sources.

  2. Energy Efficiency: Using less energy in homes, cars, and industries.

  3. Planting Trees: Trees absorb CO2 from the air.

  4. Sustainable Farming: Farming in ways that reduce emissions and capture carbon.

  5. Laws and Agreements: International efforts like the Paris Agreement to cut emissions.

  6. Education and Awareness: Teaching people about global warming and how to help.
