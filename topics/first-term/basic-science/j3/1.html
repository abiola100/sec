FAMILY TRAITS


Family traits refer to physical, genetic, and behavioral characteristics passed from one generation to another within a family.





TYPES OF FAMILY TRAITS


  1. Physical Traits: Features like height, skin color, eye color, and hair type.

  2. Behavioral Traits: Habits, talents, or abilities, like being artistic or athletic.

  3. Health Traits: Genetic conditions or tendencies, like diabetes or allergies.

  4. Facial Features: Similarities in nose shape, mouth shape, or dimples.

  5. Blood Type: A trait inherited directly from parents, such as blood group A, B, AB, or O.



Dominant and recessive


Dominant and recessive traits are types of genes that are passed from parents to children.

A dominant trait is shown if at least one dominant gene is inherited from either parent, while a recessive trait only appears if a child inherits the recessive gene from both parents.




Dominant trait A trait that appears when at least one dominant gene is inherited. For example, brown eyes dominate over blue eyes.




Recessive trait A trait that appears only when two recessive genes are inherited. For example, blue eyes appear only if both parents pass the recessive gene.
