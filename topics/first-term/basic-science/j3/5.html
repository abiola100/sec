EFFECT OF FLOODING ON COMMUNITY AND FARM LAND
  1. Property Damage: Homes, buildings, and infrastructure like roads and bridges can be destroyed or severely damaged.

  2. Loss of Life: Flooding can lead to fatalities due to drowning or related accidents.

  3. Health Risks: Stagnant water can breed diseases such as malaria and cholera, and contaminated water supplies can cause health issues.

  4. Displacement: Communities may need to evacuate, leading to temporary or permanent displacement of residents.

  5. Economic Loss: Flooding disrupts local economies, causing losses in businesses, agriculture, and livelihoods.

  6. Crop Destruction: Floods can wash away or submerge crops, leading to loss of harvests and food shortages.

  7. Soil Degradation: Farmland may suffer from erosion, loss of fertile topsoil, and reduced soil quality, affecting future agricultural productivity.



  8. Livestock Loss: Floodwaters can drown livestock or lead to diseases, reducing the number of animals for farming.

  9. Infrastructure Damage: Irrigation systems, fences, and farm buildings can be damaged, impacting agricultural operations.

  10. Environmental Impact: Flooding can lead to the destruction of natural habitats, pollution of waterways, and loss of biodiversity.
