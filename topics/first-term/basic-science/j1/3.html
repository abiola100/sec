DRUG ABUSE


What is drug abuse?


Drug abuse can be define as the intentional use of legal or illegal drugs in a manner that is harmful to one's health or well-being.



COMMON EXAMPLES OF DRUG ABUSE IN NIGERIA


  1. Alcohol: Widely used and often abused across various demographics.




  2. Cannabis (Marijuana): The most commonly used illicit drug.




  3. Prescription opioids and painkillers: Such as Tramadol and codeine-based cough syrups.




  4. Stimulants: Including amphetamines and cocaine.






  5. Inhalants: Such as glue, petrol, and other volatile solvents.

  6. Benzodiazepines: Prescription medications like Valium and Rohypnol.

  7. Synthetic cannabinoids: Often marketed as "Spice" or "K2."


EFFECT OF DRUG ABUSE


  1. Drug abuse can cause serious damage to the heart, liver, and kidneys.

  2. Continuous use can lead to physical and psychological dependence on drugs.

  3. It can lead to depression, anxiety, and other mental disorders.

  4. Drug abuse can affect decision-making and lead to risky behaviors.

  5. It can cause problems in family, work, and social relationships.

  6. Long-term abuse can shorten life expectancy due to health complications.

  7. The cost of purchasing drugs can lead to financial strain.

  8. Drug abuse can result in legal problems, including arrests and imprisonment.

  9. Drug abuse weakens the immune system, making the body more vulnerable to diseases.
