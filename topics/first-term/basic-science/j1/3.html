DRUG ABUSE

What is drug abuse?

Drug abuse can be define as the intentional use of legal or illegal drugs in a manner that is harmful to one's health or well-being.



COMMON EXAMPLES OF DRUG ABUSE IN NIGERIA

  1. Alcohol: Widely used and often abused across various demographics.




  2. Cannabis (Marijuana): The most commonly used illicit drug.




  3. Prescription opioids and painkillers: Such as Tramadol and codeine-based cough syrups.




  4. Stimulants: Including amphetamines and cocaine.






  5. Inhalants: Such as glue, petrol, and other volatile solvents.

  6. Benzodiazepines: Prescription medications like Valium and Rohypnol.

  7. Synthetic cannabinoids: Often marketed as "Spice" or "K2."


EFFECT OF DRUG ABUSE
  1. Physical Health Issues: Heart disease, liver damage, respiratory problems, and increased risk of infectious diseases.

  2. Mental Health Problems: Anxiety, depression, paranoia, and hallucinations.

  3. Addiction: Compulsive drug seeking and use despite harmful consequences.

  4. Cognitive Impairment: Memory loss, impaired judgment, and reduced concentration.

  5. Behavioral Changes: Aggression, erratic behavior, and social withdrawal.

  6. Legal Issues: Arrests, imprisonment, and legal penalties.

  7. Financial Problems: Loss of income, job instability, and financial instability.

  8. Relationship Strain: Conflicts with family, friends, and colleagues.

  9. Overdose: Risk of fatal overdose leading to death.
