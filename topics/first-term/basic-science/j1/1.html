SANITATION

What is sanitatation ?

Sanitation refers to the systems and practices used to maintain clean and hygienic conditions, particularly through the safe disposal of human waste, garbage, and wastewater, to prevent disease and ensure public health.



IMPORTANCE OF SANITATION
  1. Prevents Disease: Reduces the spread of infectious diseases and improves public health.

  2. Improves Quality of Life: Enhances living conditions and overall well-being.

  3. Environmental Protection: Prevents contamination of water bodies and soil, protecting ecosystems.

  4. Economic Benefits: Reduces healthcare costs and increases productivity.

  5. Social Dignity: Provides dignity and safety, especially for vulnerable populations like women and children.


METHOD OF SANITATION
  1. Sewage Treatment: Processes to treat wastewater before disposal or reuse.




  2. Solid Waste Management: Collection, disposal, recycling, and treatment of solid waste.




  3. Hygiene Promotion: Education and practices to promote personal and community hygiene.




  4. Water Treatment: Processes to purify water for drinking and other uses.




  5. Sanitation Infrastructure: Construction and maintenance of toilets, latrines, and drainage systems.




  6. Vector Control: Measures to control insects and other organisms that transmit diseases, such as mosquito control.




  7. Handwashing Facilities: Provision of facilities and promotion of handwashing with soap and water to prevent the spread of diseases.
