LIVING THINGS AND NON LIVING THING


Living thing:

What is living thing?

A living thing, or organism, can be define as any entity that has life.


Examples
  1. Humans: People who can think and talk.

  2. Trees: Big plants with trunks and branches.

  3. Mushrooms: Fungi that grow from the ground.

  4. Bacteria: Tiny organisms that can live in our bodies.




CHARACTERISTICS OF LIVING THING


  1. Movement: Living things can move, either by changing location or moving parts of their body.




  2. Respiration: They can break down food to release energy.




  3. Nutrition: They need food to provide energy and materials for growth and repair.




  4. Irritability (Response to stimuli): They can respond to changes in their environment.




  5. Growth: Living things grow and develop over time.




  6. Excretion: They can remove waste products from their bodies.




  7. Reproduction: They can produce new individuals of the same species.




  8. Development: They undergo changes throughout their life cycle.


Non-Living thing:

What is Non-living thing?

a non-living thing can be define as an object or material that does not have life.

Examples:
  1. Rocks: Hard, natural objects found on the ground.

  2. Water: A liquid that we drink and use for many purposes.

  3. Air: The invisible gas we breathe.

  4. Table: A piece of furniture with a flat top and legs.


CHARACTERISTICS OF NON-LIVING THING


  1. They do not increase in size or change over time on their own.

  2. They do not consume food or produce energy.

  3. They cannot produce offspring or make more of themselves.

  4. They do not react to changes in their environment.

  5. They cannot move on their own.

  6. They are not made up of cells.

  7. They do not adapt to their environment over time.
