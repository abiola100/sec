Translation of Word Problems into Numerical Expressions (Expanded Note)

Meaning of Word Problem

A word problem is a story that describes something happening with numbers — like buying, sharing, adding, or removing things. Your job is to change the story into a mathematical sentence (called a numerical expression) so you can find the answer.

Meaning of Numerical Expression

A numerical expression is a sentence made up of numbers and math signs (like +, −, ×, ÷). It helps us to show the story in mathematics form.

Examples:

5 + 3
12 − 4 + 2
6 × 3 − 5

Steps in Translating Word Problems

  1. Read the story carefully.

  2. Find all the numbers in the question.

  3. Look for keywords that show the operation to use.

  4. Write the mathematical (numerical) expression using the right signs.

  5. Solve the expression step by step.

  6. Write your final answer clearly.

Keywords That Show Operations

Operation Keywords (Words That Show It) Example
Addition (+) sum, total, together, in all, more than “She bought 5 more…”
Subtraction (−) left, gave away, remain, less than “He gave away 3…”
Multiplication (×) times, each, altogether, product “Each costs 4…”
Division (÷) shared, equally, per, divided among “Shared among 5…”

Examples

Example 1

Word Problem:
Chika had 8 pencils. She bought 6 more and then gave 3 to her brother. How many pencils does she have now?

Step 1: Numbers → 8, 6, 3

Step 2: Operations → bought more (add), gave away (subtract)

Step 3: Expression → 8 + 6 − 3

Step 4: Solve → 8 + 6 = 14, 14 − 3 = 11

Answer: Chika now has 11 pencils.

Example 2

Word Problem:
A carton contains 5 packets of biscuits. Each packet has 10 biscuits. If Tunde eats 8 biscuits, how many are left?

Step 1: Numbers → 5, 10, 8

Step 2: Operations → multiplication (packets × biscuits), subtraction (ate)

Step 3: Expression → (5 × 10) − 8

Step 4: Solve → 50 − 8 = 42

Answer: There are 42 biscuits left.

Example 3

Word Problem:
A teacher bought 4 boxes of chalk. Each box has 6 sticks. She gave 5 sticks to her friend and 3 sticks to her pupils. How many sticks remain?

Step 1: Numbers → 4, 6, 5, 3

Step 2: Operations → multiplication, subtraction

Step 3: Expression → (4 × 6) − 5 − 3

Step 4: Solve → 24 − 5 = 19, 19 − 3 = 16

Answer: 16 sticks remain.

Example 4

Word Problem:
There are 12 mangoes. They are shared equally among 3 children. Each child eats 2 mangoes and keeps the rest. How many mangoes does each child keep?

Step 1: Numbers → 12, 3, 2

Step 2: Operations → division, subtraction

Step 3: Expression → (12 ÷ 3) − 2

Step 4: Solve → 4 − 2 = 2

Answer: Each child keeps 2 mangoes.

Example 5

Word Problem:
Ngozi had ₦20. She bought a pen for ₦5, a ruler for ₦3, and later got ₦10 from her friend. How much does she have now?

Step 1: Numbers → 20, 5, 3, 10

Step 2: Operations → subtraction (spent), addition (received)

Step 3: Expression → 20 − 5 − 3 + 10

Step 4: Solve → 20 − 5 = 15, 15 − 3 = 12, 12 + 10 = 22

Answer: Ngozi now has ₦22.

How to Think of It (Simple for a 6-Year-Old)

Think of word problems as stories with hidden math inside. Your job is to:

  1. Find the numbers in the story.

  2. Find the action words (like “gave,” “bought,” “shared”).

  3. Turn them into numbers and math signs (+, −, ×, ÷).

  4. Do the math step by step.

Summary

  1. A word problem is a story that needs a mathematical answer.

  2. We translate it into a numerical expression (a math sentence).

  3. Keywords tell us whether to add, subtract, multiply, or divide.

  4. Always arrange the numbers correctly before solving.

  5. After solving, write your answer in a sentence form.
