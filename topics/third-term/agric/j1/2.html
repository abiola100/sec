WEEDS AND PEST CONTROL




what is Weeds ?

  Weeds are unwanted plants that grow where they are not wanted, often in gardens, lawns, or fields. They can take nutrients and water away from the plants you want to grow.




CHARACTERISTICS OF WEEDS

  1. Weeds grow quickly, often faster than other plants.

  2. They can survive in tough conditions and poor soil.

  3. Weeds produce lots of seeds, which helps them spread easily.

  4. Weeds take nutrients, water, and sunlight away from other plants.

  5. They are hard to get rid of and can regrow from small pieces.

  6. Weeds can adapt to different environments and conditions, making them difficult to control.

  7. Many weeds have deep or extensive root systems that help them access water and nutrients and make them hard to remove completely.

  8. Some weeds release chemicals into the soil that inhibit the growth of other plants.

  9. Weeds can often withstand herbicides and other control methods, sometimes becoming resistant over time.

  10. Weeds come in many shapes and sizes, including grasses, broadleaf plants, and even some aquatic plants, allowing them to thrive in various settings.
