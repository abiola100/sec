DAMAGE THAT CAN BE DONE BY CROP PEST
  1. Eating Leaves: Insects like caterpillars and beetles chew up plant leaves, which makes it hard for plants to grow because they need leaves to make food from sunlight.

  2. Damaging Fruits and Flowers: Bugs like fruit flies and stink bugs can ruin fruits and flowers, reducing the amount of harvest and making the produce less appealing.

  3. Harming Stems and Roots: Some insects, such as rootworms, eat the stems and roots of plants, making it hard for plants to get water and nutrients, which can cause the plants to die.

  4. Sucking Sap: Insects like aphids and whiteflies suck the juice from plants, making them weak, causing leaves to curl or turn yellow, and spreading plant diseases.

  5. Ruining Stored Grains: Bugs like weevils eat stored grains, reducing the amount and quality of the grain that can be used or sold.

  6. Spreading Diseases: Some insects carry diseases from one plant to another, which can infect and kill large areas of crops.

  7. Making Plants Look Bad: Insects can leave marks and scars on fruits and vegetables, making them look unattractive and harder to sell.

  8. Causing Other Infections: Damage from insects can create wounds in plants, allowing fungi and bacteria to infect them, leading to more crop loss.
