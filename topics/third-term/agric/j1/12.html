EFFECT OF PLANTS ON YIELD
  1. Eating Plants: Pests chew on leaves, stems, roots, and fruits, weakening plants and reducing crop yield.

  2. Sucking Sap: Insects like aphids and whiteflies suck plant juices, leading to stunted growth and lower yields.

  3. Root Damage: Soil pests chew on roots, impairing water and nutrient uptake, which stunts plant growth.

  4. Spreading Diseases: Many pests carry diseases that infect plants, reducing their health and yield.

  5. Secondary Infections: Pest wounds allow other infections to enter, causing more plant damage.

  6. Stress on Plants: Continuous pest attacks weaken plants, making them less productive.

  7. Appearance Damage: Pests cause cosmetic damage, making produce less marketable.

  8. Contamination: Pests can leave droppings and mold, reducing the quality and safety of produce.

  9. Higher Costs: Pest control measures increase farming expenses.

  10. Market Loss: Severe pest damage can make crops unsellable, leading to financial losses.

  11. Less Produce: Overall pest damage results in fewer crops being harvested.
