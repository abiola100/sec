CROP ROTATION

Crop rotation : can be define as the practice of growing different types of crops in the same area across different seasons or years.





IMPORTANCE OF CROP ROTATION


    1. Rotating crops helps Maintains Soil Fertility . Different crops use different nutrients, which prevents depletion and improves soil health over time.

    2. Deep-rooted plants help hold the soil in place, preventing erosion from rain and wind.

    3. Crop rotation breaks pest cycles. By switching crops, pests that target a specific crop donâ€™t have a chance to multiply and cause long-term damage.

    4. Changing crops reduces the spread of Plant Diseases. Some diseases only affect certain plants, so rotating crops helps prevent their buildup in the soil.

    5. Changing crops each season makes it harder for weeds to take over the land.

    6. Crop rotation maintains healthy soil, which leads to better crop production. By keeping the soil balanced, crops are more likely to thrive.

    7. Growing different crops offers multiple income sources. Farmers reduce the risk of loss due to crop failure or fluctuating market prices.
