FARM ANIMAL

what is farm animal;
  A farm animal can be define as domesticated animal raised for food, work, or other products like milk, eggs, or wool. Examples include cows, chickens, pigs, and sheep. They live on farms and help support agricultural activities.



VARIOUS FORMS OF FARM ANIMALS
  1. Cows: Cows are large animals raised for their milk, meat, and hides. They are often seen grazing in fields and are essential for dairy products like milk, cheese, and butter.

  2. Chickens: Chickens are birds kept for their eggs and meat. They live in coops and can be found pecking around farmyards.

  3. Pigs: Pigs are raised for their meat, known as pork. They are intelligent animals that enjoy rooting around in the dirt.

  4. Sheep: Sheep are kept for their wool, meat (lamb), and milk. They have thick, fluffy coats that are sheared off to make wool products.
  5. Goats: Goats are versatile animals raised for their milk, meat, and hides. They are known for being curious and agile, often climbing and exploring.

  6. Horses: Horses are strong animals used for riding, farm work, and transport. They are known for their speed and endurance.

  7. Ducks: Ducks are birds kept for their eggs, meat, and feathers. They are often found in ponds and enjoy swimming.

  8. Turkeys: Turkeys are large birds raised mainly for their meat, especially popular during holidays like Thanksgiving. They have distinctive feathers and a gobbling call.

  9. Rabbits: Rabbits are raised for their meat and fur. They are small, fluffy animals that reproduce quickly and are often kept in hutches.

  10. Donkeys: Donkeys are strong, hardy animals used for carrying loads and working on farms. They are known for their endurance and sure-footedness.
