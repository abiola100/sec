USES OF WEEDS



  1. Weeds can add nutrients to the soil when they break down.

  2. Weeds' roots help keep soil in place and stop it from washing away.

  3. Weeds provide food and shelter for insects, birds, and other animals.

  4. Some weeds can be used as natural medicine to treat health problems.

  5. Weeds offer nectar and pollen for bees and butterflies.

  6. Weeds can be composted to create rich soil for gardening.

  7. Weeds can show if the soil is too acidic or lacks nutrients.

  8. Cut weeds can be spread on the ground to keep soil moist and stop more weeds from growing.

  9. Weeds can be mixed into the soil to make it more fertile.

  10. Some weeds can be eaten by livestock, providing them with extra food.
