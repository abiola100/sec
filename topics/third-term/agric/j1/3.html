USES OF WEEDS
Despite often being seen as undesirable, weeds can have several useful purposes:
  1. Improve Soil: Weeds can add nutrients to the soil when they break down.

  2. Prevent Erosion: Weeds' roots help keep soil in place and stop it from washing away.

  3. Wildlife Habitat: Weeds provide food and shelter for insects, birds, and other animals.

  4. Medicinal: Some weeds can be used as natural medicine to treat health problems.

  5. Help Pollinators: Weeds offer nectar and pollen for bees and butterflies.

  6. Make Compost: Weeds can be composted to create rich soil for gardening.

  7. Soil Health Indicators: Weeds can show if the soil is too acidic or lacks nutrients.

  8. Natural Mulch: Cut weeds can be spread on the ground to keep soil moist and stop more weeds from growing.

  9. Green Manure: Weeds can be mixed into the soil to make it more fertile.

  10. Feed for Animals: Some weeds can be eaten by livestock, providing them with extra food.
