ADVERTISING IN AGRICULTURE
Meaning

Advertising in agriculture is the way of informing people about agricultural products to encourage them to buy.

It is used by farmers and agro-businesses to show the existence, quality, uses, and benefits of farm products.

Advertising helps farmers increase sales, earn more income, and reach more customers.



Purpose / Importance of Advertising
  1. To attract customers and make them aware of farm products.

  2. To create awareness about new or existing agricultural products.

  3. To increase interest in the products by highlighting benefits or special features.

  4. To increase income by promoting more sales of farm produce.

  5. To inform about quality so that customers know the product is safe and useful.


Price Determinants (Factors that Affect Price)

The price of agricultural products depends on the following:

  1. Cost of Production – Total money spent on seeds, fertilizers, labour, and transport.

  2. Supply – If there is more produce than needed, prices fall; if there is less, prices rise.

  3. Demand – Products that many people want usually sell for higher prices.

  4. Quality – High-quality produce fetches higher prices than poor-quality produce.

  5. Season – Prices are usually lower during harvest and higher in the off-season.

  6. Market Location – Products sold in towns or cities are more expensive than in villages.

  7. Government Policies – Subsidies, price controls, or export rules can affect prices.

  8. Storage and Preservation – Poor storage can force farmers to sell cheaply to avoid spoilage.


Methods of Pricing Agricultural Products
  1. Cost-Plus Pricing – Adding a profit margin to the cost of production.

  2. Competitive Pricing – Setting prices similar to other farmers in the market.

  3. Penetration Pricing – Offering lower prices to attract new buyers.

  4. Auction Pricing – Selling to the person who offers the highest price.

  5. Negotiation – Farmers and buyers agree on a price.

  6. Government Fixed Pricing – Government sets minimum or maximum prices for certain crops.


Types of Advertising in Agriculture

There are three main types of advertising:

  1. Informative Advertising – Gives information about the product, its benefits, and uses.

  2. Persuasive Advertising – Encourages people to choose a product by appealing to emotions or showing advantages over others.

  3. Competitive Advertising – Shows that one product is better than other competing products.


Forms of Advertising in Agriculture

Advertising can be done through different forms (methods):

  1. Print Media – Newspapers, magazines, flyers, pamphlets, and brochures.

  2. Radio and Television – Using mass media to reach a wide audience.

  3. Billboards and Posters – Placing large advertisements in public places.

  4. Handbills / Pamphlets – Distributing small leaflets to potential buyers.

  5. Digital / Online Advertising – Using social media, websites, or mobile apps.

  6. Mobile Phones / SMS – Sending messages directly to customers.

  7. Direct Advertising – Communicating directly through email, catalogues, or promotional letters.

  8. Indirect Advertising – Promoting products indirectly through sponsorship, exhibitions, or product placement.


Information Usually Given in Agricultural Advertisement
  1. The name of the product.

  2. The quality or special features of the product.

  3. Where to buy the product or contact details.

  4. Price or payment methods (optional).


Advantages / Benefits of Advertising in Agriculture
  1. Increased Sales – Advertising helps reach more customers and sell more products.

  2. Better Market Reach – Farmers can reach distant or new markets.

  3. Brand Building – Helps create a reputation or “brand” for farm produce.

  4. Customer Education – Shows buyers how to use the product or its benefits.

  5. Competitive Edge – Helps differentiate a product from competitors’ products.


Challenges / Constraints of Advertising in Agriculture
  1. High Cost – Advertising can be expensive, especially through TV, radio, or print media.

  2. Limited Access to Media – Rural farmers may not have access to radio, internet, or newspapers.

  3. Low Literacy – Customers may not read print advertisements.

  4. Poor Infrastructure – Lack of electricity, internet, or phones limits advertising options.

  5. Mistrust – Buyers may not trust the advertisement unless the product is proven.

  6. Maintenance of Message – Running repeated advertisement campaigns can be difficult.


Strategies to Improve Agricultural Advertising
  1. Use local and affordable media like community radio or notice boards.

  2. Use digital tools like WhatsApp, Facebook, or SMS to reach more people cheaply.

  3. Work in groups or cooperatives to share advertising costs.

  4. Use good pictures or videos to show the quality of produce.

  5. Repeat advertisements regularly to help customers remember the product.

  6. Collect feedback to improve future advertisements.


Summary

Advertising in agriculture is used to inform, persuade, and attract customers to farm products.

It helps farmers increase sales, earn income, and reach more markets.

There are different types (informative, persuasive, competitive) and forms (print, radio, digital, direct, indirect) of advertising.

Advertising helps build reputation, educate customers, and compete effectively, but it may be expensive and challenging.

Effective advertising requires proper planning, repeated campaigns, and using the most suitable methods for the target audience.
