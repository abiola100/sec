CLASSES OF FISH
  • Osteichthyes (Bony fish)

  • Chondrichthyes (Cartilaginous fish)

  • Agnatha (Jawless fish)


    1. Osteichthyes (Bony fish): Fish with a bony skeleton, such as tilapia, catfish, and salmon.

    2. Chondrichthyes (Cartilaginous fish): Fish with a cartilage skeleton, such as sharks and rays.

    3. Agnatha (Jawless fish): Fish without jaws, such as lampreys and hagfish.



    characteristics of Osteichthyes (Bony Fish)


    1. Have a bony skeleton.

    2. Possess paired fins and a swim bladder for buoyancy.

    3. Scales cover their bodies.

    4. Most species have gills for breathing.




    characteristics of Chondrichthyes (Cartilaginous Fish)


    1. Have a skeleton made of cartilage, not bone.

    2. Possess rough, tooth-like scales (placoid scales).

    3. Lack a swim bladder but rely on liver oil for buoyancy.

    4. They breathe using gills and have multiple gill slits.




    characteristics of Agnatha (Jawless Fish):


    1. Lack jaws and paired fins.

    2. Have a smooth, slimy skin and no scales.

    3. Their skeleton is made of cartilage.

    4. They are often parasitic or scavengers.
