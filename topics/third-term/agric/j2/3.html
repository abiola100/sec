CLASSES OF FISH


Based on Habitat



  1. Freshwater Fish : Live in rivers, lakes, streams, and ponds. They have adaptations to deal with varying oxygen levels and temperatures.Examples: Trout, Bass, Catfish.

  2. Saltwater Fish : Inhabit oceans and seas. They often have adaptations to maintain osmotic balance in salty water.Examples: Tuna, Clownfish, Mackerel.

  3. Brackish Water Fish : Found in estuaries, where freshwater mixes with saltwater. These fish can tolerate a wide range of salinity.Examples: Mangrove Killifish, Mudskippers.




Based on Morphology



  1. Flatfish : Have a flattened body, with both eyes on one side of the head. They are adapted to a benthic lifestyle, lying on the ocean floor. Examples: Flounder, Sole, Halibut.

  2. Eel-like Fish : Have elongated, snake-like bodies. They can navigate through narrow spaces and often burrow into substrates.Examples: Eels, Lampreys, Hagfish.

  3. Deep-sea Fish : Adapted to the extreme pressures, low temperatures, and darkness of the deep ocean. Often have bioluminescent features and specialized feeding mechanisms. Examples: Anglerfish, Gulper Eel.

  4. Pelagic Fish :Live in the open ocean, away from the sea floor. They often have streamlined bodies for efficient swimming over long distances.




Based on Feeding Habits



  1. Herbivorous Fish :Feed primarily on plant material, algae, and phytoplankton. Often have specialized teeth for scraping or nibbling plants.Examples: Parrotfish, Surgeonfish.

  2. Carnivorous Fish : Predators that feed on other fish or marine animals. They have sharp teeth and strong jaws for capturing and consuming prey. Examples: Sharks, Barracudas, Piranhas

  3. Omnivorous Fish : Eat a varied diet that includes plants, invertebrates, and smaller fish. They are adaptable to different food sources. Examples: Carp, Tilapia.



Based on Reproductive Strategies


  1. Oviparous Fish : Lay eggs that develop and hatch outside the mother's body. Eggs can be laid in nests, attached to substrates, or released into the water column. Examples: Most Bony Fish (e.g., Salmon, Clownfish).

  2. Viviparous Fish : Give birth to live young. The embryos develop inside the mother's body and receive nutrients directly from her. Examples: Some Sharks, Guppies.

  3. Ovoviviparous Fish : Retain eggs within the mother's body until they hatch, so she gives birth to live young. The embryos are nourished by the egg yolk. Examples: Certain Sharks (e.g., Sand Tiger Shark).



Based on Other Types of Aquatic Organisms



  1. Symbiotic Fish : Engage in mutualistic relationships with other species. Clownfish receive protection from anemones, while cleaner wrasse feed on parasites from larger fish. Examples: Clownfish (with sea anemones), Cleaner Wrasse.

  2. Parasitic Fish :Feed on the bodily fluids of other fish. They often attach to the host using specialized mouthparts.Examples: Lampreys.

  3. Benthic Fish : Live and feed on or near the bottom of water bodies. They often have adaptations for a benthic lifestyle, such as flattened bodies or burrowing behavior.Examples: Gobies, Flatfish.
