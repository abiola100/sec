FORESTS AND FOREST USES



What is forest ?

Forests can be define as complex ecosystems that provide a wide range of ecological, economic, and social benefits.




TYPES OF FOREST



  1. Tropical Rainforests : Dense, evergreen forests found near the equator, characterized by high rainfall and biodiversity.

  2. Temperate Forests : Deciduous or mixed forests found in temperate regions, with distinct seasons and moderate rainfall.

  3. Boreal Forests (Taiga) : Coniferous forests located in northern regions, with cold climates and long winters.

  4. Tropical Dry Forests : Forests with seasonal rainfall and a distinct dry season, often found in tropical regions.

  5. Montane Forests : Forests found in mountainous regions, characterized by varying climates and vegetation with altitude.

  6. Mangrove Forests : Coastal forests found in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by salt-tolerant trees and unique ecosystems.

  7. Savanna Woodlands : Open forests with scattered trees and grasses, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions with seasonal rainfall.

  8. Temperate Rainforests : Dense, moist forests located in temperate regions, with high rainfall and rich biodiversity.

  9. Mediterranean Forests : Forests characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters, found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

  10. Subtropical Forests : Forests located in subtropical regions, often with high humidity and diverse plant species, including both evergreen and deciduous trees.
