FOREST RESOURCES


What is forest resources ?

Forest resources are the diverse materials and services provided by forests, which support economic, ecological, and cultural values crucial for human well-being and environmental sustainability.



USES OF FOREST RESOURCES
  1. Timber and Wood Products : Used for construction, furniture, paper, and fuelwood.

  2. Non-Timber Forest Products (NTFPs) : Provide food (fruits, nuts), medicine (plants), and materials (resins, fibers).

  3. Biodiversity Conservation: Preserving habitats and species diversity.

  4. Ecosystem Services : Carbon sequestration, water regulation, soil stabilization, and climate moderation.

  5. Recreation and Tourism : Offering opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing.

  6. Cultural and Spiritual Values : Supporting traditions, rituals, and indigenous knowledge.

  7. Economic Benefits : Providing livelihoods through forestry, tourism, and sustainable use practices.

  8. Climate Change Mitigation : Forests play a crucial role in mitigating climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

  9. Water Resource Management : Forests help regulate water flow, improve water quality, and recharge groundwater, benefiting agriculture and urban areas downstream.
