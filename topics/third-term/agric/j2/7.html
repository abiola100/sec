FOREST RESOURCES


What is forest resources ?

Forest resources are the diverse materials and services provided by forests, which support economic, ecological, and cultural values crucial for human well-being and environmental sustainability.






USES OF FOREST RESOURCES


  1. Used for construction, furniture, paper, and fuelwood.

  2. Provide food (fruits, nuts), medicine (plants), and materials (resins, fibers).

  3. Preserving habitats and species diversity.

  4. Carbon sequestration, water regulation, soil stabilization, and climate moderation.

  5. Offering opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing.

  6. Supporting traditions, rituals, and indigenous knowledge.

  7. Providing livelihoods through forestry, tourism, and sustainable use practices.

  8. Forests play a crucial role in mitigating climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

  9. Forests help regulate water flow, improve water quality, and recharge groundwater, benefiting agriculture and urban areas downstream.
