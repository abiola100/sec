EFFECT OF FOREST ON THE ENVIRONMENT
  1. Climate Regulation : Forests absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, helping to mitigate climate change and regulate global temperatures.

  2. Biodiversity Support : They provide habitats for a wide variety of plant and animal species, maintaining ecological balance and genetic diversity.

  3. Water Cycle Regulation : Forests enhance precipitation, influence local and regional weather patterns, and help maintain the water cycle by intercepting rainfall, reducing runoff, and promoting groundwater recharge.

  4. Soil Conservation : Tree roots stabilize soil, preventing erosion and maintaining soil health and fertility.

  5. Air Quality Improvement : Forests filter pollutants from the air, contributing to cleaner, healthier air for surrounding communities.

  6. Flood and Drought Mitigation : Forests help to moderate the effects of extreme weather events by absorbing excess rainfall and maintaining moisture levels during dry periods.

  7. Nutrient Cycling : Forests play a critical role in the cycling of nutrients, returning essential elements to the soil through the decomposition of organic matter, which supports plant growth and ecosystem productivity.

  8. Urban Heat Island Effect Reduction : Forests and urban green spaces help to cool urban areas, reducing the urban heat island effect by providing shade and releasing moisture into the air through transpiration.
