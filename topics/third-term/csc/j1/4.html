CLASSIFYING INFORMATION BY MODE OF TRANSMITION


Information can be classified by modes of transmission into several categories based on how it is conveyed from one person or system to another. Here are some common modes of transmission for information:


  1. Verbal Transmission: This involves the spoken word, whether face-to-face, over the phone, or through voice recordings. It is direct and allows immediate feedback, making it effective for clarifications.

  2. Non-Verbal Transmission: Information is shared through body language, facial expressions, gestures, or tone of voice. It often complements verbal communication and can convey emotions or attitudes.

  3. Written Transmission: Written information is transmitted through text formats, such as letters, emails, reports, or books. It is precise and can be referenced later, making it ideal for documentation.

  4. Visual Transmission: This mode uses images, videos, charts, and graphics to convey information. It is effective for illustrating concepts, data, or processes that benefit from visual representation.

  5. Digital/Electronic Transmission: Information is transmitted electronically through digital media, like emails, social media, or online messaging. It allows instant, widespread communication and data sharing.

  6. Broadcast Transmission: Information is transmitted to a large audience through radio, television, or online streaming. This mode is suitable for reaching many people quickly with general information.

  7. Face-to-Face Transmission: Direct, in-person transmission where participants share information in the same location. This mode allows for immediate clarification, feedback, and relationship-building.

  8. Print Transmission: Information shared through printed materials, like newspapers, magazines, and flyers. This mode is suitable for mass dissemination and can be circulated in physical locations.
