INFORMATION TRANSMITION


information transmission refers to the process of sending or sharing information from one person or place to another.

It covers how people communicate and exchange ideas, knowledge, or messages to ensure that information reaches the intended person or group.


INFORMATION TRANSMITION


Information can be transmitted through two primary means: electronic and non-electronic.


  1. Electronic Transmission: This involves sharing information through digital or electronic devices. Electronic transmission is typically faster and allows information to reach large or distant audiences almost instantly. Common forms include:

    • Emails: Sending written messages and attachments online.

    • Social Media: Platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram allow instant sharing of messages, images, and videos.

    • Television and Radio: Broadcasting information to the public, accessible through TVs and radios.

    • Mobile Phones: Using calls, text messages, and apps to communicate directly.

    • Internet Platforms: Websites, blogs, and online forums allow information sharing across global audiences.



  2. Non-Electronic Transmission: This involves traditional, non-digital ways of sharing information. Although often slower, it can be highly effective in certain settings, especially where electronic means are unavailable. Examples include:

    • Face-to-Face Communication: Direct in-person conversations or meetings.

    • Written Documents: Physical letters, reports, notices, or memos.

    • Print Media: Newspapers, magazines, and posters, useful for local or specific audiences.

    • Body Language and Gestures: Non-verbal cues used to convey messages in person.
