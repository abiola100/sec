DRUG TRAFFICKING
Meaning of drug trafficking

Drug trafficking is the illegal selling, transporting, or distributing of drugs that are prohibited by law. Examples of such drugs include marijuana (Indian hemp), cocaine, heroin, and tramadol.




Causes of Drug Trafficking
  1. Poverty – Lack of money may push people into illegal business.

  2. Unemployment – No jobs can lead people to sell drugs for survival.

  3. Greed – Desire to make quick money.

  4. Ignorance – Not knowing the dangers or consequences.

  5. Peer pressure – Influence from friends or bad company.



Dangers / Consequences of Drug Trafficking
  1. Crime Increase – Leads to theft, armed robbery, and other criminal activities.

  2. Health Problems – Drug abuse causes addiction, mental illness, and sometimes death.

  3. Family Breakdown – Families may suffer when members abuse drugs or are arrested.

  4. Economic Loss – Reduces productivity and slows national development.

  5. Social Disorder – Communities become unsafe and unstable.

  6. Loss of Respect – Individuals and communities involved in drugs may face stigma.



Solutions to Drug Trafficking
  1. Education and Awareness – Teach the public, especially youths, about dangers of drugs.

  2. Strict Law Enforcement – Punish drug traffickers to deter others.

  3. Community Support Programs – Provide jobs and support for vulnerable people.

  4. International Cooperation – Work with other countries to stop drug supply.

  5. Good Parenting – Families should guide children to avoid drugs.

  6. Rehabilitation Centres – Help drug users recover and reintegrate into society.
