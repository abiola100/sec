SOCIAL PROBLEMS IN NIGERIA
Meaning of Social Problems

Social problems are the various harmful conditions or behaviours that affect a large number of people in society. They disturb the peace, progress, and wellbeing of individuals and the community as a whole. A social problem is any situation that many people consider undesirable and want to change.

Social problems can also be define as any issue or condition in society that negatively affects the well-being of individuals and groups, and makes it difficult for people to live harmoniously. Social problems usually result from human behaviour, poor management of resources, or breakdown of societal values. They can affect families, communities, and the nation as a whole.




Examples of Social Problems in Nigeria
  1. Poverty – A condition where people do not have enough money to meet their basic needs like food, shelter, and clothing.

  2. Corruption – Dishonest acts by people in authority, such as taking bribes or misusing public funds.

  3. Unemployment – When people who are willing and able to work cannot find jobs.

  4. Illiteracy – The inability to read and write, which limits opportunities in life.

  5. Drug Abuse – The misuse of drugs or taking harmful substances, leading to health and social issues.

  6. Prostitution – Engaging in sexual activity for money.

  7. Armed Robbery – Stealing from people with the use of weapons.

  8. Kidnapping – The unlawful taking of someone against their will, usually for ransom.

  9. Hooliganism – Violent and destructive behaviour, especially during sports events or public gatherings.

  10. Terrorism – The use of violence to create fear and achieve political, religious, or social aims.




Causes of Social Problems in Nigeria
  1. Poverty – When people lack basic needs, they may turn to crime or other harmful acts.

  2. Unemployment – Joblessness often pushes people into illegal activities.

  3. Bad Governance – Poor leadership and corruption increase social problems.

  4. Poor Parenting – Children without proper upbringing may develop bad habits.

  5. Peer Pressure – Friends can influence individuals into drug abuse, stealing, or other wrong acts.

  6. Lack of Education – Illiteracy makes it difficult for people to get good jobs or make wise decisions.

  7. Breakdown of Law and Order – When laws are not enforced, crimes increase.



Effects of Social Problems in Nigeria
  1. Insecurity – People live in fear due to crime and violence.

  2. Loss of Lives and Property – Social problems like robbery and terrorism destroy lives and belongings.

  3. Poor Image of the Country – Nigeria is seen negatively by other countries.

  4. Hindrance to Development – Investors avoid countries with high crime rates.

  5. Family Instability – Crimes, drug abuse, and unemployment can break families apart.

  6. Increase in Prison Population – More people are jailed due to criminal activities.



Solutions to Social Problems in Nigeria
  1. Provision of Jobs – Creating employment opportunities for the youth.

  2. Good Governance – Leaders should be honest and committed to development.

  3. Law Enforcement – The police and other agencies should enforce laws strictly.

  4. Public Enlightenment – Educating citizens on the dangers of social problems.

  5. Encouraging Education – Providing free or affordable education for all.

  6. Empowering Youths – Skill acquisition programmes to keep youths productive.

  7. Eradicating Corruption – Punishing corrupt officials and discouraging bribery.


  8. Participation in Civil Society – Join groups and organizations that promote peace and development.

  9. Personal Discipline – Be honest, law-abiding, and avoid harmful behaviours.
