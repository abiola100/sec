RENEWABLE AND NON-RENEWABLE ENERGY


Renewable energy

Renewable Energy: can be define as the Energy from sources that won't run out and can be used over and over, like sunlight, wind, and water.


examples
  1. Solar Energy: Power from the sun.

  2. Wind Energy: Power from wind using turbines.

  3. Hydropower: Power from flowing water.

  4. Biomass Energy: Power from plant and animal materials.

  5. Geothermal Energy: Power from heat inside the Earth.



USES OF RENEWABLE ENERGY


  1. Solar Energy: Powering buildings and heating water.

  2. Wind Energy: Generating electricity with wind turbines.

  3. Hydropower: Producing electricity from flowing water.

  4. Biomass Energy: Creating electricity, heat, and biofuels from organic materials.

  5. Geothermal Energy: Heating buildings and generating electricity from Earth's heat.




IMPLICATION OF MISUSE OF RENEWABLE ENERGY



  1. Environmental Damage: Improper use can still harm ecosystems (e.g., large-scale biofuel production).

  2. Resource Strain: Overuse of water for hydropower can impact water availability.

  3. Waste Generation: Disposal of old solar panels and wind turbines can create waste issues.

  4. Land Use: Large renewable energy projects can take up significant land and affect local wildlife.

  5. Economic Costs: Poor planning and inefficient use can lead to higher costs and wasted resources.



Non-Renewable energy

Non-Renewable Energy: can be define as the Energy from sources that can run out and can't be replaced quickly, like coal, oil, and natural gas.


examples
  1. Coal: A black or brownish-black rock used as fuel.

  2. Oil: Liquid fossil fuel used to make gasoline and other products.

  3. Natural Gas: Gaseous fossil fuel used for heating and electricity.

  4. Nuclear Energy: Power from splitting atoms, usually uranium.



USES OF NON-RENEWABLE ENERGY


  1. Coal: Generating electricity in power plants.

  2. Oil: Producing fuels like gasoline and diesel for transportation.

  3. Natural Gas: Heating homes and generating electricity.

  4. Nuclear Energy: Producing electricity in nuclear power plants.

  5. Propane: Fueling heating systems, stoves, and engines.




IMPLICATION OF MISUSE OF NON-RENEWABLE ENERGY


  1. Environmental Damage: More pollution and harm to nature.

  2. Climate Change: Warming the planet and causing extreme weather.

  3. Resource Depletion: Running out of limited energy sources.

  4. Economic Impact:More health problems from pollution.

  5. Economic Impact: Higher energy costs and economic problems.
