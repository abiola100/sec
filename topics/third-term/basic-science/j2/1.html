THERMAL ENERGY


Thermal energy, also known as heat energy : is a form of energy that is associated with the temperature of an object. The higher the temperature of the object, the more thermal energy it possesses.





ACTIVITIES THAT ILLUSTRATE DIFFERENT ASPECT OF THERMAL ENERGY


Here are a few activities that illustrate different aspects of thermal energy, including heat transfer, thermal expansion etc



  1. Heat Transfer : - Conduction, Convection, and Radiation

    2. Objective:

    To demonstrate the three modes of heat transfer: conduction, convection, and radiation.

    Materials Needed: Metal spoon, Cup of hot water, Paper cup, Candle, Aluminum foil, Ice cubes.

    1. Conduction:
      • Place the metal spoon in a cup of hot water. After a minute or two, touch the end of the spoon that is not in the water.

      • Discuss how the heat from the hot water is transferred to the metal spoon through conduction.


    2. Convection:


      • Fill a paper cup with water and add a few drops of food coloring. Place an ice cube in the center of the cup.

      • Observe the movement of the colored water as it cools and sinks, showing convection currents.

      • Alternatively, you can use a hairdryer to blow warm air over the surface of the water and observe the movement.


    3. Radiation :


      • Light a candle and hold your hand near (but not too close to) the flame. Feel the warmth on your hand.

      • Wrap a piece of aluminum foil around another candle and compare the heat felt with and without the foil. Discuss how radiation transfers heat.


  2. Thermal Expansion

    3. Objective : To illustrate the concept of thermal expansion.

    Materials Needed : Metal ball and ring apparatus ,Bunsen burner or candle,Ice water

    • Initial Observation :
      • Pass the metal ball through the ring to show that it fits at room temperature.

    • Heating the Ball
      • Heat the metal ball using a Bunsen burner or candle for a few minutes.

      • Try to pass the heated ball through the ring again. Observe that the ball no longer fits due to thermal expansion.
    • Cooling the Ball:
          • Place the heated ball in ice water for a few minutes.

      • Try to pass the ball through the ring again. Observe that it fits once more after cooling.
