TYPES OF PETROLEUM PRODUCTS

Petroleum products are derived from crude oil through the refining process and are used for various applications. Here are some common types of petroleum products:

  1. Gasoline (Petrol): Used primarily as fuel in internal combustion engines for cars, motorcycles, and light trucks.

  2. Diesel: Used as fuel for diesel engines in trucks, buses, boats, trains, and heavy machinery.

  3. Kerosene: Used as jet fuel, heating fuel, and for lamps and cooking in some regions.

  4. Jet Fuel: A specialized type of kerosene used in aircraft engines.

  5. Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG): A mixture of propane and butane used for heating, cooking, and as fuel for certain vehicles.

  6. Fuel Oil: Used for heating and for power generation in some industrial applications.

  7. Lubricants: Including motor oil, grease, and other oils used to reduce friction in engines and machinery.

  8. Asphalt (Bitumen): Used in road construction and roofing.

  9. Naphtha: Used as a feedstock for petrochemical production, as a solvent, and in gasoline blending.

  10. Paraffin Wax: Used in candles, packaging, cosmetics, and as a moisture barrier.

  11. Petrochemical Feedstocks: Includes ethylene, propylene, and butadiene, which are used to produce plastics, synthetic rubber, and other chemicals.

  12. Coke: A solid carbonaceous material derived from refining used in industrial processes, such as in steelmaking.

  13. Residual Fuel Oil: The heavy fraction remaining after the distillation of crude oil, used in power plants, ships, and large industrial plants.
