IMPLICATION OF BAD SCIENTIFIC PRACTICE
  1. Loss of Trust: Erodes public trust in science and scientific institutions.

  2. Harm to Individuals: Can lead to harm or injury to participants in research studies.

  3. Wasted Resources: Misallocation of funding and resources on invalid or fraudulent research.

  4. Policy Misguidance: Influences policymakers based on inaccurate or misleading information.

  5. Hindered Progress: Slows down genuine scientific and technological advancements.

  6. Legal Consequences: Can lead to lawsuits, fines, and loss of professional licenses.

  7. Health Risks: Can result in unsafe medical treatments or public health practices.

  8. Environmental Damage: May lead to harmful environmental practices and policies.

  9. Academic Repercussions: Damages the reputation of academic institutions and researchers.

  10. Ethical Violations: Breaches ethical standards, leading to exploitation and unfair treatment of subjects.
