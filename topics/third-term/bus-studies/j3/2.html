BALANCE SHEET

A balance sheet is a financial statement that presents a company's financial position at a specific point in time.


COMPONENTS OF BALANCE SHEET
  1. Assets: What the company owns.


    2. Current Assets: Cash, inventory, accounts receivable (money owed to the company).

    Non-current Assets: Long-term items like property and equipment.


  2. Liabilities: What the company owes.

    3. Current Liabilities: Debts due within a year (like accounts payable).

    Non-current Liabilities: Long-term debts (like loans).

  3. Equity: Owner's share in the company.

    4. Includes common stock and retained earnings (profits not paid out as dividends).



Example of a Simple Balance Sheet

ABC Company Balance Sheet

As of December 31, 2024

| Total Liabilities and Equity | 250,000 |




USES OF BALANCE SHEET
  1. balance sheet Shows the company's ability to pay off debts.

  2. balance sheet helps investors to evaluate the company’s worth.

  3. balance sheet assists lenders in deciding loan approval.

  4. balance sheet helps in monitoring growth or decline over time.

  5. balance sheet evaluates how efficiently a company uses its assets.

  6. Identifies financial risks and liabilities.

  7. balance sheet ensures that, company meets legal and regulatory requirements.

    Equity
