JOURNALS

Meaning of journal :

journal can be define as process whereby The initial occurrence of financial transactions is recorded in one place.



TYPES OF JOURNALS


  1. General Journal: Accounts for each and every kind of financial transaction as they happen.

  2. Sales Journal: Records each and every sale transacted on credit.

  3. Purchases Journal: Accounts for all type of purchase made on credit.

  4. Cash Receipts Journal: Documents incoming cash flow.

  5. Cash Payments Journal: Records any out going cash flow.



FORMS OF JOURNALS


  1. Paper Ledger: A hand written book where transactions are written down

  2. Computerized Ledger: Electronic accounts kept with software specifically made for bookkeeping

  3. Specialized Ledger: Documents created for certain operations e.g sale or purchase ledgers with fixed columns
