PERIMETER OF REGULAR POLYGONS
DEFINITION

The perimeter of a plane shape is the total distance around the boundary of the shape.

For polygons, the perimeter is found by adding the lengths of all the sides.

A regular polygon is a polygon whose sides and angles are all equal.

Example: Square, Equilateral triangle, Regular hexagon, etc.




FORMULAE FOR PERIMETER

Square:

  • All 4 sides equal.

  • Perimeter P = 4s

+------+
|      |
|      |
+------+

(s = side length)

Rectangle:

  • Opposite sides equal.

  • Perimeter P = 2(l + b)

+----------+
|          |
|          |
+----------+

(l = length, b = breadth)

Triangle:

  • If equilateral (regular): P = 3a

  • If scalene or isosceles: add all sides.

   /\
  /  \
 /____\

(a = side of equilateral triangle)

Trapezium:

  • Perimeter = sum of all 4 sides.

  • Only special trapeziums (isosceles) can be considered “regular-like”.

  +------+
 /        \
+----------+

Parallelogram:

  • Opposite sides equal.

  • Perimeter P = 2(a + b)

  /------/
 /      /
/------/

Circle:

  • Circle has no sides, but its “perimeter” is called circumference.

  • C = 2πr or C = πd

WORKED EXAMPLES

Example 1: Square
Find the perimeter of a square with side 12 cm.

Solution:

P = 4s

= 4 × 12

= 48 cm

Answer: 48 cm

Example 2: Rectangle

A rectangle is 15 cm long and 8 cm wide. Find its perimeter.

Solution:

P = 2(l + b)

= 2(15 + 8)

= 2 × 23

= 46 cm

Answer: 46 cm

Example 3: Equilateral Triangle

Each side of an equilateral triangle is 7 cm. Find its perimeter.

Solution:

P = 3a

= 3 × 7

= 21 cm

Answer: 21 cm

Example 4: Trapezium

The sides of a trapezium are 10 cm, 8 cm, 7 cm and 9 cm. Find its perimeter.

Solution:
P = a + b + c + d

= 10 + 8 + 7 + 9

= 34 cm

Answer: 34 cm

Example 5: Parallelogram

A parallelogram has adjacent sides 12 cm and 9 cm. Find its perimeter.

Solution:

P = 2(a + b)

= 2(12 + 9)

= 2 × 21

= 42 cm

Answer: 42 cm

Example 6: Circle (Circumference)

Find the circumference of a circle of radius 14 cm. Use π = 22/7.

Solution:

C = 2πr

= 2 × 22/7 × 14

= 2 × 44

= 88 cm

Answer: 88 cm

SUMMARY

  • Perimeter means the distance around a shape.

  • For polygons, add up the sides.

  • For regular polygons, multiply the number of sides by the side length.

  • Circle’s perimeter is called circumference.

  • Formulae:

  • Square: 4s

  • Rectangle: 2(l + b)

  • Triangle: 3a (if equilateral)

  • Parallelogram: 2(a + b)

  • Trapezium: a + b + c + d

  • Circle: 2πr or πd
