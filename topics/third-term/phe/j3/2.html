Non contact game

Non-contact sports are games where players do not physically touch opponents during play. These sports focus on skills, speed, and accuracy rather than strength or physical collisions.


Examples of Non-Contact Sports
  1. Athletics (Running, Jumping)

  2. Swimming

  3. Badminton

  4. Tennis

  5. Table Tennis

  6. Volleyball (modified as non-contact)

  7. Gymnastics


Athletics (Track and Field Events)

  1. Track Events:

    • Sprint races (100m, 200m)

    • Middle-distance (400m, 800m)

    • Relay races (4×100m)

  2. Field Events:

    • Long Jump

    • High Jump

    • Shot Pu



Basic Skills for Athletics (Track and Field Events)

  • Proper starting stance

  • Arm and leg coordination

  • Breathing techniques (for long races)



Rules for Athletics (Track and Field Events)

  • No false starts (disqualification after 1 false start)

  • Stay in your lane (for sprint races)

  • Jump/throw within marked areas



Equipment for Track and Field Events

  • Running spikes

  • Starting blocks

  • Baton (for relays)

  • Measuring tape (for jumps)



SWIMMING

Swimming can be define as propelling oneself through water using arms and legs.


Types of Swimming Strokes

  • Freestyle

  • Breaststroke

  • Backstroke

  • Butterfly



Basic Skills in Swimming

  1. Proper breathing technique

  2. Arm and leg coordination

  3. Floating



Rules in Swimming

  1. Start with a dive (except backstroke)

  2. Stay in assigned lane

  3. Touch the wall at turns



Swimming equipment

  1. Swimwear

  2. Goggles

  3. Swim cap




Racket Sports (Badminton, Tennis, Table Tennis)
Sport Court Size Scoring Key Rules
1 Badminton 13.4m × 6.1m 21 points Serve diagonally; shuttle must land in bounds
2 Tennis 23.8m × 11m 15-30-40-game Ball must bounce once per side
3 Table Tennis 2.7m × 1.5m 11 points Serve must bounce on both sides



Common Skills

  • Hand-eye coordination

  • Footwork

  • Serving techniques



Safety Measures

  • Warm-up before any activity

  • Use proper footwear (e.g., running shoes for athletics)

  • Follow pool rules for swimming (no running, no diving in shallow ends)

  • Check equipment condition (e.g., racket strings, tennis balls)




GYMNASTICS

Gymnastics is a sport combining strength, flexibility, balance, and coordination through systematic exercises.


Types of Gymnastics

  1. Artistic: Apparatus routines (most common in schools).

  2. Rhythmic: Dance + ribbon/hoop manipulation.

  3. Trampoline: Acrobatics on springed beds.

  4. Aerobic: Music-based endurance routines.

  5. Acrobatic: Partner lifts and balances.



Key Apparatus & Events for Gymnastics

  1. Floor Exercise: 12m×12m padded mat for tumbling/dance routines.

  2. Vault: Springboard and vaulting table for aerial maneuvers.

  3. Balance Beam: 10cm-wide, 5m-long beam for precision routines.

  4. Parallel Bars: Two horizontal bars for swinging/balancing (men's event).

  5. Uneven Bars: Two staggered bars for women's dynamic routines.



Fundamental Skills

  • Rolls: Forward roll, backward roll

  • Balances: Handstand, headstand

  • Rotations: Cartwheel, round-off

  • Advanced: Back handspring, aerial walkover



Safety Measures in Gymnastics

  1. Always warm up for 15-20 minutes.

  2. Use spotters for new skills.

  3. Inspect mats/apparatus before use.

  4. Wear fitted clothing without zippers.

  5. Progress from simple to complex moves.



Benefits of Gymnastics

  • Physical: Builds muscle strength and bone density

  • Mental: Enhances focus and discipline

  • Social: Promotes teamwork in group routines



Common Injuries & Prevention

  • Sprains: Prevent with proper landing practice

  • Fractures: Avoid by using crash mats

  • Strains: Warm-up/stretching prevents this
