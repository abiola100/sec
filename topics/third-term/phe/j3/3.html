FLOOR ACTIVITIES

Definition

Floor activities are gym exercises done on mats to build strength and flexibility. They include moves like rolls and handstands.



Equipment Needed for Floor activities
  1. Floor Mat (12m × 12m padded surface).

  2. Safety Mats (for landing).

  3. Springboard (for take-off in some moves).



Basic Floor activities
  1. Forward Roll - Bend down, put hands on mat, tuck your head, roll forward like a ball, and stand up.

  2. Backward Roll - Squat low, roll backwards, push with your hands, and finish standing.

  3. Cartwheel - Move sideways like a wheel: hand-hand-foot-foot.

  4. Handstand - Kick up onto your hands with straight arms and legs.

  5. Headstand - Balance upside-down on your head and hands.


Advanced Skills in floor activities
  1. Round-off (Cartwheel with 180° turn).

  2. Back Walkover (Backward arching motion).

  3. Front Handspring (Forward flip with hand support).

  4. Aerial Cartwheel (No-hands cartwheel).



Safety Rules in floor activities
  1. Always warm up for 10-15 minutes

  2. Use proper landing techniques (bent knees).

  3. Have a spotter for new skills.

  4. Remove all jewelry before practice.

  5. Progress from simple to complex moves.



Benefits of Floor Exercises
  1. Improves flexibility and balance.

  2. Strengthens arms, legs and core.

  3. Develops body awareness.

  4. Boosts confidence in physical abilities.

  5. Enhances coordination and rhythm.



Common Mistakes to Avoid in flor exercises
  • Arching back during rolls

  • Landing with locked knees.

  • Attempting advanced skills without basics.

  • Not using spotters for new moves.

  • Rushing through movements.



Teaching Progression for floor exercises
  1. Start with static balances (e.g., tuck stand)

  2. Move to rolling exercises.

  3. Introduce weight transfers (cartwheels).

  4. Progress to flight elements (handsprings)
