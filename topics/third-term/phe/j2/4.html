Fresh Approach to School Health Programme
Meaning of FRESH

The word FRESH is an acronym that means:

  • F - Focusing
  • R - Resources
  • E - on Effective
  • S - School
  • H - Health

So, FRESH stands for: "Focusing Resources on Effective School Health."

It is a modern and improved method of promoting health in schools. The FRESH approach was introduced by international health organizations such as UNESCO, UNICEF, WHO, and the World Bank to help children, especially in developing countries, grow up healthy, stay in school, and learn better.


Definition

The FRESH approach can be defined as a method of improving student health through clean surroundings and health care.



pillars of the FRESH Approach
  • Health-Related School Policies: These are rules in school that help keep students safe and healthy, like washing hands and no smoking.

  • Safe and Supportive School Environment: This means keeping the school clean, safe, and friendly for everyone to learn well.

  • Skills-Based Health Education: Teaching students how to stay healthy by learning about hygiene, nutrition, and disease prevention.

  • School-Based Health and Nutrition Services: Providing health care like first aid, vaccinations, and meals to help students stay strong and well.
