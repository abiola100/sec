REHEARSAL
meaning of Rehearsal

Rehearsal is the process of practising a play or performance before it is shown to an audience. It is an important part of theatre and other stage activities because it helps actors and the production team prepare for the actual show.

Rehearsal allows everyone to learn their parts, improve their acting, and make sure everything runs smoothly.




Stages of Rehearsal

Rehearsal is usually done in stages. Each stage helps the actors and crew to get ready for the final show.

  1. Reading Rehearsal: Actors read the script aloud together to understand the story and their lines. No acting or movement happens at this stage.

  2. Blocking Rehearsal: The director instructs actors where to stand, move, and when to enter or exit the stage. Movements and gestures are practised.

  3. Technical Rehearsal: Actors perform while lights, sound, music, and scene changes are tested to match the action on stage.

  4. Dress Rehearsal: The full run-through with costumes, makeup, and props as if it is the actual performance. This helps actors prepare fully.



Importance of Rehearsal
  1. It helps actors to Learn and remember their lines and movements.

  2. It helps actors to Understand their characters better.

  3. It helps actors to Improve their acting skills.

  4. It helps actors to Coordinate with other actors and the technical team.

  5. It helps actors to Reduce nervousness and build confidence.

  6. It helps actors to Ensure the performance is smooth and enjoyable.



Qualities Needed During Rehearsal
  1. Punctuality: Always coming on time.

  2. Discipline: Listening and following instructions carefully.

  3. Focus: Concentrating on learning and practising.

  4. Cooperation: Working well with others in the team.

  5. Patience: Accepting corrections and practising until perfect.



Common Mistakes to Avoid During Rehearsal
  1. Being late or absent.

  2. Forgetting lines or ignoring the script.

  3. Not listening to the director.

  4. Making noise or disturbing others.

  5. Not using costumes or props properly.




People Involved in Rehearsa
  • Director: Leads the rehearsal and instructs the actors.

  • Actors: Perform their parts and learn through practice.

  • Stage Manager: Organises rehearsals and manages the stage.

  • Technical Crew: Handle lighting, sound, props, and scene changes.
