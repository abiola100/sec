UNITY
Meaning of Unity

unity is the act of working together as a group or a team. When people are united, they live in peace, help one another, and work towards a common goal.

Unity brings about understanding, cooperation, and progress among individuals and groups in the family, school, community, and the nation. Example: When students work together during sanitation, or when classmates help each other during group work, that is unity.




Types of Unity
  1. Unity in the Family: This is when all family members love, care for, and support one another. It helps the family to live in peace.

  2. Unity in the School: This is when teachers, students, and school workers work together in harmony for the progress of the school.

  3. Unity in the Community: This is when people in a town or village cooperate to develop their area through community service, self-help, and mutual understanding.

  4. Unity in the Country: This is when all citizens from different tribes, cultures, and religions live in peace and work together to move the country forward.



Causes of Disunity
  1. Tribalism – When someone believes their tribe is better than others.

  2. Religious Intolerance – When people hate others because of religion.

  3. Jealousy and Hatred – When people do not like others for no good reason.

  4. Gossip and Lies – Saying what is not true about someone to cause problems.

  5. Disobedience and Disrespect – When people refuse to follow rules or respect elders.

  6. Selfishness – Thinking only about yourself without caring for others.




Ways of Promoting Unity
  1. Respect for One Another.

  2. Fairness and Justice.

  3. Religious Tolerance.

  4. Avoiding Tribalism and Favouritism.

  5. Encouraging Teamwork.

  6. Good Communication.



Importance of Unity
  1. It promotes peace and harmony.

  2. It encourages progress and development.

  3. It reduces conflict, violence, and war.

  4. It builds strong relationships and friendships.

  5. It helps in achieving national goals.

  6. It makes people feel belonging and secure.



Consequences of Disunity
  1. Fighting and Quarrels.

  2. Hatred and Bitterness.

  3. Delay in Progress.

  4. Breakdown of Relationships.

  5. Underdevelopment of Society.

  6. Division and War.



Demonstration of Acts of Unity
  1. Helping one another in times of need.

  2. Participating in group projects or community service.

  3. Supporting classmates during school events.

  4. Sharing and caring for others.

  5. Respecting people’s differences.

  6. Joining hands to clean the environment.

  7. Singing the national anthem together.

  8. Celebrating cultural festivals peacefully.




Unity in Nigerian Context
Nigeria is a multi-ethnic and multi-religious country. We have over 250 ethnic groups and 500+ languages. For Nigeria to move forward:
  1. We must accept and respect our differences.

  2. Unity must be taught from childhood.

  3. National symbols (e.g., anthem, flag, NYSC) should be used to promote unity.

  4. No tribe or religion should be treated as superior.

“Though tribe and tongue may differ, in brotherhood we stand.” — from Nigeria’s national anthem
