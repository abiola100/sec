FOOD PROCESSING
Meaning of Food processing

Food processing is the transformation of raw food materials into forms suitable for human consumption. This can involve cleaning, cutting, cooking, canning, freezing, or packaging.

  • Purpose: to make food edible, safe, and easier to store.

  • Improves taste, appearance, and shelf life.

  • Encourages variety and reduces wastage.



Importance of Food Processing
  1. Preserves food for longer periods – reduces spoilage.

  2. Makes food safe to eat – kills harmful microorganisms.

  3. Improves taste and texture – makes food appealing.

  4. Enhances nutritional value – some processing adds nutrients.

  5. Facilitates storage and transport – easier to move and sell.

  6. Reduces wastage – surplus food can be processed for later use.



Disadvantages of Food Processing
  1. Nutrient loss – overcooking or improper processing.

  2. High cost – processed foods may be expensive

  3. Artificial additives – some contain preservatives or colors.

  4. Requires equipment – machines or special tools needed.

  5. Time-consuming – some processes take longer.



Food Processing Methods

Food processing methods refer to general ways food is handled to make it suitable for consumption or storage:

  1. Mechanical Processing – cutting, grinding, peeling, slicing (e.g., grinding maize into flour).

  2. Thermal Processing – heating or cooling (boiling, steaming, frying, baking, pasteurization, canning).

  3. Fermentation – using microorganisms to improve flavor, digestibility, and shelf life (e.g., yoghurt, sourdough bread).

  4. Preservation Techniques – freezing, drying, salting, sugaring, smoking, canning.

  5. Fortification – adding nutrients (e.g., iodized salt, vitamin-enriched flour).



Food Processing Techniques
These are specific ways or steps used to process food safely and effectively:

  1. Boiling – cooking in water to make food safe and edible

  2. Steaming – cooking with steam to retain nutrients.

  3. Frying – cooking in oil for taste and texture.

  4. Baking – cooking with dry heat (bread, cakes).

  5. Pasteurization – heating liquids to kill microorganisms (milk).

  6. Canning – sealing food in airtight containers and heating.

  7. Freezing – storing at low temperatures to prevent spoilage.

  8. Drying/Dehydration – removing moisture to extend shelf life.

  9. Salting/Sugaring – inhibits bacterial growth.

  10. Smoking – preserves and flavors foods (fish, meat)



Factors to Consider When Processing Food
  1. Type of food – some foods need special methods.

  2. Purpose of processing – preservation, safety, taste improvement.

  3. Nutritional value – avoid excessive nutrient loss.

  4. Equipment available – tools or machines.

  5. Time and labor – choose suitable methods.

  6. Storage facilities – refrigeration, drying space, or containers.

  7. Hygiene and safety – prevent contamination.



Common Mistakes in Food Processing
  • Overcooking or improper processing causing nutrient loss.

  • Poor hygiene causing contamination.

  • Using wrong preservation method for the type of food.

  • Improper storage after processing.
