FOOD SAFETY
Meaning of Food Safety

Food safety is the practice of handling, preparing, and storing food in ways that prevent contamination, spoilage, and foodborne illnesses.

  • Ensures food is safe to eat and free from harmful bacteria, viruses, chemicals, or foreign objects.

  • Involves personal hygiene, proper storage, and careful preparation.



Importance of Food Safety
  1. Prevents foodborne diseases – protects the family from illnesses like diarrhea, cholera, and typhoid.

  2. Maintains nutritional value – safe handling preserves vitamins and minerals.

  3. Reduces food wastage – properly stored and handled food lasts longer.

  4. Protects vulnerable groups – children, pregnant women, and the elderly are less likely to get sick.

  5. Promotes general health – reduces risk of infections and chronic illnesses.



Perishable and Non-Perishable Foods
Perishable Foods

  • Foods that spoil quickly if not stored properly.

  • Usually high in moisture and require refrigeration.

  • Examples: meat, fish, milk, eggs, fruits, and vegetables.

Non-Perishable Foods

  • Foods that last longer and can be stored at room temperature.

  • Examples: rice, beans, garri, sugar, salt, flour, and canned foods.

Importance in Food Safety:

  • Knowing which foods are perishable or non-perishable helps in proper storage and handling.



How to Keep Food Safe
To prevent contamination and spoilage, follow these food safety practices:
  1. Cleanliness – wash hands, utensils, and surfaces regularly.

  2. Separate raw and cooked foods – prevent cross-contamination.

  3. Proper cooking – cook food thoroughly to kill harmful microorganisms.

  4. Safe storage – refrigerate perishable items and use airtight containers.

  5. Use clean water and fresh ingredients – avoid contaminated sources.

  6. Check expiry dates – do not consume expired packaged foods.

  7. Proper disposal of spoiled foods – throw away rotten or contaminated food immediately.

  8. Control pests – keep kitchens free from flies, rodents, and insects.



Consequences of Poor Food Safety
  1. Food poisoning and infections.

  2. Malnutrition due to spoiled food losing nutrients.

  3. Loss of money from wasted food.

  4. Hospitalization or death in severe cases.
