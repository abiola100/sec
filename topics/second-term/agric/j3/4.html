PEOPLE INVOLVED IN STOCK EXCHANGE


  1. Individual Investors: Private individuals investing for personal gain.

  2. Institutional Investors: Large organizations investing substantial amounts.

  3. Full-Service Brokers: Offer personalized investment advice and services.

  4. Discount Brokers: Provide low-cost trading with fewer services.

  5. Online Brokers: Platforms for trading stocks online at reduced costs.

  6. New York Stock Exchange (NYSE): Major global stock exchange.

  7. NASDAQ: Leading electronic stock exchange.

  8. Market Makers: Ensure liquidity by being ready to buy and sell stocks.

  9. Day Traders: Trade stocks within the same day.

  10. Swing Traders: Hold stocks for days to weeks for short-term gains.

  11. Algorithmic Traders: Use algorithms for rapid, criteria-based trading.

  12. Financial Analysts: Evaluate and provide stock recommendations.

  13. Financial Advisors: Offer investment strategy and specific stock advice.

  14. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC): Regulates U.S. stock markets.

  15. Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA): Oversees U.S. brokerage firms.

  16. Clearinghouses: Handle transaction settlement and delivery.

  17. Listed Companies: Companies with stocks listed on exchanges.

  18. Financial News Outlets: Provide news and analysis influencing stock prices.

  19. Data Providers: Offer market data and financial analytics tools.

  20. Custodians: Safekeep and manage securities for clients.

  21. Depositories: Hold securities in electronic form for transaction settlement.
