SOURCE DOCUMENTS IN AGRICULTURE
Meaning of source document

Source documents are original records that show that a transaction has taken place. They are used as the foundation for recording transactions in journals and ledgers. Source documents help confirm that transactions actually happened and make farm records accurate, reliable, and verifiable. Proper use of source documents ensures transparency and accountability in farm bookkeeping.



Types of Source Documents

In agricultural bookkeeping, the common types of source documents include:

  1. Sales Invoice

    Issued when farm produce is sold.

    Shows details of items sold, quantity, price per unit, total cost, and the buyer’s information.

    Used to record sales in the sales journal.

  2. Purchase Invoice

    Received when the farm buys goods or services from a supplier.

    Shows details of items purchased, quantity, price, total cost, and supplier’s information.

    Used to record purchases in the purchases journal or accounts payable.

  3. Receipt

    A proof of cash received, for example, from customers or other sources.

    Used to record cash inflows in the cash book or receipts journal.

  4. Voucher

    A document prepared to support payments made, such as payments for wages, utilities, or purchases.

    Provides information on the purpose and amount of payment.

  5. Credit Note

    Issued by a supplier or seller to reduce the amount a buyer owes.

    Used when goods are returned or when there was an overcharge.

  6. Debit Note

    Issued to increase the amount a buyer owes.

    Used when the buyer was undercharged or additional payment is required.


Relationship of source documents with Journals

After a source document is issued or received, it is used to record transactions in journals, which are the first step in the bookkeeping process.

  1. Sales Invoice → Sales Journal

  2. Purchase Invoice → Purchase Journal

  3. Receipts or Vouchers → Cash Book or Payments Journal

After recording in journals, transactions are posted to ledgers, which summarize all transactions in specific accounts, such as Sales Account, Purchases Account, or Cash Account. These ledger balances are then used to prepare farm financial statements.

Importance of Source Documents
  1. They provide evidence that transactions actually happened.

  2. They prevent errors in recording transactions.

  3. They help organize farm records systematically.

  4. They provide information for auditing and verification.

  5. They help farmers manage resources and make informed decisions.

Summary

  • Source documents are essential for proof of all farm transactions.

  • They form the foundation for recording in journals and ledgers.

  • They prevent errors and help organize farm records.

  • They improve transparency and accountability in farm management.

  • Proper use of source documents helps farmers plan, track resources, and make informed decisions.
