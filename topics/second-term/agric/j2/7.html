EFFECT OF DISEASE ON FARM ANIMAL
  1. Reduced Productivity : Diseases in farm animals can lead to lower milk yield, egg production, and weight gain, directly impacting the farmer's income.

  2. Increased Mortality : Severe diseases can cause high mortality rates among livestock, leading to significant losses and reduced herd or flock size.

  3. Economic Losses: Veterinary costs, decreased market value of diseased animals, and increased feed requirements for sick animals can lead to substantial financial burdens on farmers.

  4. Spread of Infection: Contagious diseases can rapidly spread among animals, causing widespread outbreaks that are difficult and costly to control.

  5. Food Safety Concerns: Diseases in farm animals can lead to contamination of animal products, posing serious health risks to consumers and potential market bans.

  6. Breeding Disruptions: Illnesses can affect reproductive health, leading to reduced fertility rates, birth defects, and difficulties in maintaining a stable breeding program.

  7. Animal Welfare Issues: Diseases often cause significant pain and distress to animals, raising concerns about their well-being and necessitating improved animal care practices.

  8. Trade Restrictions: Outbreaks of certain animal diseases can lead to quarantine measures and trade restrictions, affecting the ability to sell animals and animal products both domestically and internationally.
