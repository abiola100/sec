FARM ANIMAL DISEASES

Farm animal diseases refer to illnesses that affect domesticated animals raised for agricultural purposes, such as cattle, sheep, pigs, poultry, and goats.



Common farm animal diseases
  1. Foot-and-Mouth Disease : Highly contagious viral disease affecting cloven-hoofed animals such as cattle, pigs, sheep, and goats, causing fever and blisters in the mouth and feet.

  2. Avian Influenza (Bird Flu) : Viral infection primarily affecting birds, often spread through wild bird migrations, leading to severe respiratory distress and significant mortality in poultry.

  3. Mastitis: Common bacterial infection in dairy cows, causing inflammation of the udder tissue, reduced milk production, and sometimes severe discomfort or pain for the animal.

  4. Scrapie : A fatal, degenerative disease affecting sheep and goats, characterized by severe itching, abnormal behavior, and progressive loss of coordination.

  5. Bovine Tuberculosis (TB) : Chronic bacterial infection in cattle caused by Mycobacterium bovis, leading to persistent cough, weight loss, and potential spread to humans through consumption of unpasteurized dairy products.

  6. Swine Dysentery : A bacterial infection in pigs, causing severe diarrhea, dehydration, and potential death, primarily affecting weaned pigs and growing swine.

  7. Chicken Cholera: A highly contagious bacterial disease in poultry, causing sudden death, fever, diarrhea, and respiratory distress, often spread through contaminated feed or water.

  8. Bluetongue: A viral disease affecting sheep and other ruminants, transmitted by midges, leading to fever, swelling of the tongue, and lameness, with severe cases causing death.
