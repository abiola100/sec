MODE OF TRANSMISSION OF FARM ANIMAL DISEASES
  1. Direct Contact: Diseases spread through physical contact between animals, including touching, biting, or grooming each other.

  2. Indirect Contact: Pathogens transmitted via contaminated objects, surfaces, or environments, such as equipment, clothing, or bedding.

  3. Airborne Transmission: Pathogens spread through respiratory droplets or dust particles inhaled by animals.

  4. Vector-Borne Transmission: Diseases transmitted by insect vectors like mosquitoes, flies, ticks, or fleas, or by rodents.

  5. Ingestion: Pathogens ingested through contaminated feed, water, or by consuming infected carcasses or waste.

  6. Vertical Transmission: Pathogens passed from a pregnant animal to its fetus through the placenta.

  7. Iatrogenic Transmission: Diseases spread through medical procedures such as injections or surgeries.

  8. Sexual Transmission: Pathogens transmitted through sexual contact during mating.
