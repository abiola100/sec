COMMON SYMPTOMS OF FARM ANIMAL DISEASES


  1. Changes in Behavior : Animals may exhibit changes such as lethargy, increased or decreased activity, isolation from the herd or flock, or aggression.

  2. Loss of Appetite : Reduced or complete lack of interest in food or water.

  3. Weight Loss : Unexplained weight loss despite normal feeding practices.

  4. Respiratory Symptoms : Coughing, sneezing, nasal discharge, or difficulty breathing.

  5. Digestive Issues : Diarrhea, constipation, bloating, or abdominal pain.

  6. Fever : Elevated body temperature, often detected by touch or with a thermometer.

  7. Skin and Coat Changes : Rashes, lesions, hair loss, or changes in coat appearance.

  8. Swelling : Visible swelling of joints, limbs, or other parts of the body.

  9. Neurological : Tremors, seizures, circling behavior, or paralysis.

  10. Reproductive Issues : Abortions, infertility, or other reproductive abnormalities.

  11. Changes in Milk Production : Decreased milk yield or changes in milk quality.

  12. Discharge : Any abnormal discharge from eyes, nose, mouth, or reproductive organs.
