ANIMAL PEST CONTROL


Animal pest control refers to the management and regulation of animals that are considered pests due to their impact on human activities, health, or the environment.This includes various methods and techniques aimed at preventing or reducing the population of pest animals that may cause harm or nuisance.

Some common examples of animal pests : include rodents (like rats and mice), insects (such as mosquitoes or termites), birds (like pigeons or seagulls), and larger animals (such as raccoons, squirrels, or deer) that can cause damage to crops, property, or spread diseases.

ANIMAL PEST CONTROL METHODS
  1. Trapping and removal : Using traps to capture and relocate pests to a more suitable location away from human habitation.

  2. Chemical control : The use of pesticides or other chemical substances to kill or deter pests.

  3. Biological control : Introducing natural predators or pathogens to control pest populations.

  4. Habitat modification : Altering the environment to make it less attractive or accessible to pests.

  5. Exclusion : Using barriers or fencing to prevent pests from entering specific areas.
