CLASSIFICATION OF FARM ANIMALS BASED ON SIZE



  1. Small farm animals: Small farm animals typically refer to animals that are relatively compact in size and are manageable for small-scale farming operations. They are often raised for various purposes such as eggs, meat, or fiber. Examples of small farm animals include chickens, ducks, geese, turkeys, rabbits, and sometimes smaller breeds of pigs or goats.



  2. Medium farm animals: Medium farm animals are larger than small farm animals but still manageable for moderate-sized farming operations. They may require more space and resources compared to small animals but are still feasible for many farmers to raise. Examples of medium farm animals include sheep, goats, larger breeds of pigs, alpacas, and llamas.


  3. Large farm animals: Large farm animals are typically the largest animals raised on farms and require significant space, resources, and infrastructure to raise. They often yield substantial amounts of meat, milk, or other products. Examples of large farm animals include cows, horses, donkeys, bison, and yaks. These animals may also serve purposes beyond food production, such as draft power or companionship.
