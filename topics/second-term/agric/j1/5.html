MAMMAS AND NON MAMMAS FARM ANIMALS


  • A mammal : is an animal with warm blood and fur that gives birth to live babies and feeds them milk. Examples include cows, pigs, and sheep.



    • Characteristics of Mammals as Farm Animals
    1. Mammals have a coat of hair covering their bodies.

    2. Females Mammals produce milk to feed their young ones.

    3. Most give birth to live young, not eggs.

    4. Maintain a constant body temperature.

    5. Mammals Provide meat, milk, wool, and hides.

    6. They are easily tamed and managed.






  • Non-mammal farm animals : are animals that lay eggs or have scales instead of fur. Examples include chickens (birds) and fish in farm ponds.



    • Characteristics of Non-Mammals as Farm Animals


    1. Non-mammals reproduce by laying eggs instead of giving live birth. Example :Chickens, ducks, and reptiles.

    2. Their bodies are covered with feathers, scales, or smooth skin. Examples: Birds and fish.

    3. They cannot control their body temperature, which changes with the environment. Examples : Reptiles and amphibians.

    4. Many non-mammals produce a large number of offspring quickly. Examples : Hens and fish.

    5. Non-mammals provide valuable products like food, feathers, or materials. Examples : Chickens (eggs), fish (meat), crocodiles (skin).

    6. They can adapt to different environments and climates. Examples : Guinea fowls, tilapia, frogs.
