Singing
Meaning of Singing

Singing is the act of using the voice to produce musical tones in a rhythmic and melodic way. It is one of the most natural and expressive forms of music.

Singing involves the control of breath, pitch, tone, and timing. It can be done solo (alone), in a group (choir), or with instrumental accompaniment.




Importance of Singing
  1. Singing helps in emotional expression and relieves stress.

  2. It is used in education, worship, celebrations, and ceremonies.

  3. Singing promotes unity and cultural identity.

  4. It builds confidence and develops memory and listening skills.

  5. It teaches language, values, and social skills.



Types of Songs
  1. Patriotic songs ‚Äì e.g., National Anthem, National Pledge.

  2. Folk songs ‚Äì Traditional songs from different Nigerian ethnic groups.

  3. School songs ‚Äì School anthem, welcome songs.

  4. Moral/social songs ‚Äì Songs that teach values like honesty, unity, respect.

  5. Religious songs ‚Äì Christian or Islamic songs of worship.

How to Teach the Nigerian National Anthem ("Nigeria, We Hail Thee") in Class
  1. Begin by telling the students that the anthem has been changed and explain its history briefly.

  2. Write the full lyrics of the first stanza on the board.

  3. Explain the meaning of key words like ‚Äúhail,‚Äù ‚Äúnative land,‚Äù ‚Äúsovereign,‚Äù and ‚Äúbrotherhood.‚Äù

  4. Play or sing the anthem for them to hear the melody and rhythm.

  5. Teach them the tonic solfa line by line, clapping the rhythm as they sing.

  6. Practice each line with both solfa and words until they get the correct notes.

  7. Group the class into small teams to rehearse and sing together.

  8. Correct mistakes gently and give positive feedback.

  9. Let the full class stand and sing the anthem proudly with good posture.

  10. Repeat the performance regularly so they master the lyrics and tune confidently.

üé∂Meaning of Running Scale

A running scale is the continuous singing or playing of a set of musical notes in order, either going up (ascending) or down (descending), without stopping between the notes.

It helps to train the voice and ear to recognize pitch and improve vocal control.



üé∂What is a Musical Scale?

A musical scale is a sequence of notes arranged in a specific order of pitch. The most common scale in music is the Diatonic Scale, which contains 7 notes (Do, Re, Mi, Fa, So, La, Ti, Do).




üé∂Tonic Solfa (Ascending & Descending)

The solfa syllables used in running scale are:

  • Ascending: Do ‚Äì Re ‚Äì Mi ‚Äì Fa ‚Äì So ‚Äì La ‚Äì Ti ‚Äì Do

  • Descending: Do ‚Äì Ti ‚Äì La ‚Äì So ‚Äì Fa ‚Äì Mi ‚Äì Re ‚Äì Do

This is called a Major Scale in tonic solfa.




Purpose of Running Scale

  1. It improves vocal strength and flexibility.

  2. It trains students to sing on key and stay in pitch.

  3. It builds confidence when singing solos or in a group.

  4. It helps in ear training (hearing and identifying correct musical notes).

  5. It prepares students for more advanced singing or instrumental training.



How to Teach Running Scale in Class (Step-by-Step)

  1. Start with breathing exercises to relax the voice.

  2. Write the solfa notes on the board.

  3. Sing the scale yourself as a model.

  4. Use a keyboard or pitch pipe to give starting note.

  5. Teach each note in order ‚Äî slowly and clearly.

  6. Ask students to repeat the scale in unison.

  7. Practice both ascending and descending scale.

  8. Introduce hand signs (Curwen hand signs) if available.

  9. Let students try individually or in small groups.

  10. Encourage daily practice to improve fluency.

Pitch Identification and Differences
What is Pitch?

Pitch in music is the highness or lowness of a sound. It tells us whether a note sounds high like a whistle or low like a drum.

Pitch depends on how fast or slow a sound vibrates ‚Äî faster vibrations make high pitch, slower vibrations make low pitch.



Types of Pitch

There are two major types of pitch in music:

  1. High Pitch ‚Äì Sounds that are sharp, light, or squeaky (e.g. a whistle, bird song).

  2. Low Pitch ‚Äì Sounds that are deep or heavy (e.g. a bass drum, a man‚Äôs deep voice).



Pitch Identification

Pitch identification is the ability to recognize and tell the difference between high and low sounds. It is also about knowing the position of a note on the music staff (lines and spaces).

How to Identify Pitch
  1. By Listening: Compare sounds ‚Äì is one higher or lower than the other?

  2. With Instruments: Use instruments like piano or recorder to hear pitch differences.

  3. Using Tonic Solfa: Solfa syllables help students recognize pitch order ‚ÄîDo ‚Äì Re ‚Äì Mi ‚Äì Fa ‚Äì So ‚Äì La ‚Äì Ti ‚Äì Do (from low to high).

  4. With Musical Staff: Notes higher on the staff sound higher; lower notes on the staff sound lower.



Pitch on the Musical Staff

  • The staff has five lines and four spaces.

  • Notes placed higher on the staff are higher in pitch .

  • Notes placed lower are lower in pitch .

  • Sing ascending and descending solfa (Do ‚Äì Re ‚Äì Mi... and back).

Benefits of Learning Pitch
  1. Helps to sing or play correct notes.

  2. Trains the ear to listen carefully.

  3. Improves music reading skills.

  4. Builds a foundation for sight-singing and harmony.




VOICE TRAINING EXERCISE
Introduction

Voice training is the process of training and preparing the voice for singing or speaking. It involves exercises that help to improve the quality of the voice, increase vocal strength, and make pronunciation clearer.

It also helps to develop the ability to sing in tune and with confidence. Voice training is very important for singers, actors, broadcasters, teachers, and other professionals who use their voice every day.




Reasons for Voice Training Exercise
  1. It helps the singer to sing with a strong and pleasant voice.

  2. It improves the way the voice sounds and helps in proper voice projection.

  3. It helps in breathing well while singing or speaking.

  4. It prevents strain or damage to the voice.

  5. It increases the vocal range (ability to sing high and low notes).

  6. It makes words clear when singing or talking.

  7. It builds confidence in performance.

  5. Warm up your voice gently before every practice.
