COMPUTER ROOM MANAGEMENT ETHICS

computer room management ethics refer to guidelines for using computer labs responsibly.


  1. Maintain a dust-free environment: Regular cleaning and preventing dust buildup helps protect computer components and maintain equipment performance.

  2. Clean computers with a soft cloth: Using soft materials ensures the screen and other parts are not scratched or damaged during cleaning.

  3. Use dust covers for computers: When not in use, covering computers prevents dust accumulation and protects them from environmental damage.

  4. Install fire alarms and extinguishers: These safety measures help quickly respond to potential fires, protecting both the equipment and users.

  5. Provide adequate lighting: Sufficient lighting reduces the strain on eyes, ensuring comfort and clear visibility when working on computers.

  6. Ensure proper airflow: Good ventilation allows computers to operate without overheating, improving their longevity and performance.

  7. Ensure continuous power supply: An uninterrupted power source prevents data loss and equipment damage during power outages.

  8. Offer comfortable seating: Comfortable chairs promote better posture and help reduce discomfort during long periods of computer use.
