USES OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY IN TIMING AND CONTROL


The uses of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in timing and control refer to the application of technology to manage, regulate, and optimise processes that require precise timing and coordination. This includes automating schedules, controlling industrial processes, managing access to secure areas, monitoring energy consumption, overseeing traffic systems, and managing inventory.include:


  1. ICT helps plan meetings, reminders, and workflows for better time management.

  2. ICT tools observe time spent on activities, helping improve productivity.

  3. ICT coordinates machines in factories to enhance timing and efficiency.

  4. ICT manages secure entry times for offices, banks, and restricted areas.

  5. ICT oversees supply levels in real-time to keep supplies organised.

  6. ICT manages traffic lights and monitors road conditions to reduce congestion in busy cities.

  7. ICT systems adjust air conditioning and ventilation in offices based on time and temperature.

  8. ICT helps save energy by scheduling lights and devices to operate only when necessary.
