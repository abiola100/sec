DIGITAL DIVIDE


What is Digital Divide?



Digital Divide refers to the gap between individuals or groups who have access to modern digital technologies (like computers, smartphones, and the internet) and those who do not .

Includes inequality in : Physical access (devices, internet connections), Skills and knowledge needed to use digital tools effectively



Those Who have access vs. Those who does not have Access


Those with access typically include :


  1. Educated people

  2. Urban dwellers

  3. People from developed countries

  4. High-income earners

  5. Individuals with digital skills



Those without access include:


  1. Uneducated persons

  2. Rural residents

  3. People in less industrialized nations

  4. Low-income earners

  5. Those lacking digital skills



Factors Causing the Divide


  1. high cost of devices and data

  2. lack of infrastructure in rural areas

  3. fear of technology, cultural beliefs

  4. illiteracy or low digital literacy

  5. lack of interest or awareness



Bridging the Divide


  1. Provide infrastructure – extend broadband to rural zones

  2. Subsidize the cost of computers, tablets.

  3. Train users on basic computing and internet safety .

  4. Create digital inclusion programs such as e‑learning, computer centers in schools/libraries .

  5. Government & NGOs collaboration – initiatives like CANi (Computers for All Nigerians) .

  6. Promote gender balance by encouraging female participation in ICT.
