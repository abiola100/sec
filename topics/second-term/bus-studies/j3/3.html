CONSUMER RIGHTS AND REDRESS
Meaning of Consumer Rights

Consumer rights refer to the legal and moral entitlements that protect consumers when they buy goods and services. They are the powers and freedoms given to consumers to ensure that they are treated fairly, receive good value for their money, and are protected from harmful or dishonest business practices.

In Nigeria, these rights are supported by the Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC) and other agencies that ensure fair dealings between producers, sellers, and consumers.

It helps you to ensure that businesses act responsibly and honestly in the marketplace.



SIX BASIC RIGHTS OF CONSUMERS

1. Right to Safety

Consumers have the right to be protected against goods and services that are dangerous or harmful to health and life.

Example: Drugs, food, and electrical appliances must be tested and approved for safety before sale.

Agencies like NAFDAC and SON help to ensure this.

2. Right to Be Informed

Consumers have the right to correct and complete information about products and services before buying them.

Example: Labels must show expiry dates, ingredients, prices, and usage instructions.

3. Right to Choose

Consumers have the right to select from a variety of goods and services at fair prices.

Producers should not create monopolies that force consumers to buy only one brand.

4. Right to Be Heard

Consumers have the right to express their complaints and opinions about goods or services.

Their complaints should be fairly considered by companies and consumer protection agencies.

5. Right to Seek Redress

Consumers have the right to demand compensation, refund, or replacement when they are cheated, injured, or receive faulty goods or poor services.

Example: Returning a damaged product for refund or repair.

6. Right to Consumer Education

Consumers have the right to be educated about their responsibilities, rights, and how to make wise purchasing decisions.

Schools, media, and consumer organisations help in educating the public.



Meaning of Consumer Redress

Consumer redress means the actions or remedies available to a consumer to correct a wrong or unfair treatment suffered when buying goods or services. It is the process of seeking justice or compensation when a consumer’s right has been violated.

It helps you to make sure that consumers are not cheated and that producers take responsibility for their actions.



WAYS OF SEEKING CONSUMER REDRESS

When a consumer’s right is violated, the following steps can be taken to seek redress:

  1. Complain to the Seller or Producer: The consumer should first report the problem politely to the seller or producer.
    Example: Returning a defective phone to the shop where it was bought.

  2. Write a Formal Complaint Letter: If the seller refuses to help, the consumer should write a complaint letter stating the problem, what is expected (refund, repair, or replacement), and attach proof such as receipts.

  3. Report to Consumer Protection Agencies: The complaint can be taken to government agencies like:
    1. Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC)

    2. Standards Organisation of Nigeria (SON)

    3. National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC)

    4. Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC)

    5. Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) Consumer Protection Department


  4. Report to Consumer Rights Organisations or NGOs: There are non-governmental bodies like Consumer Advocacy Foundation of Nigeria (CAFON) and Consumer Awareness Organisation (CAO) that help consumers seek justice.

  5. Use the Media: The press, radio, television, and social media can be used to draw attention to consumer problems and force companies to act responsibly.

  6. Seek Legal Action (Court of Law): As a last resort, consumers can take legal action against the seller or company to demand compensation or punishment for the offender.


IMPORTANCE OF CONSUMER RIGHTS AND REDRESS
  1. It helps you to ensure fairness and honesty in business transactions.

  2. It helps you to protect consumers from exploitation, fraud, and unsafe products.

  3. It helps you to build consumer confidence in the market.

  4. It helps you to make producers and sellers more responsible and careful.

  5. It helps you to promote healthy competition among businesses.

  6. It helps you to strengthen the economy through fair trade practices.


BENEFITS OF PROVIDING REDRESS
  1. It helps you to build customer trust and loyalty.

  2. It helps you to improve a company’s reputation and public image.

  3. It helps you to prevent future complaints and improve product quality.

  4. It helps you to encourage fair and transparent business practices.

  5. It helps you to avoid legal actions or penalties from consumer protection agencies.

  6. It helps you to increase consumer satisfaction and confidence in the marketplace.


Problems Consumers Face in Seeking Redress

Common problems include:

  1. Ignorance of consumer rights.

  2. Fear of embarrassment or delay in the complaint process.

  3. Lack of cooperation from sellers or producers.

  4. Corruption or delay in government agencies.

  5. High cost of going to court.

  6. Poor record-keeping by consumers.


Ways of Promoting Consumer Protection and Redress in Nigeria

Ways include:

  1. Educating the public about their rights and responsibilities.

  2. Encouraging the establishment of consumer clubs in schools.

  3. Strengthening consumer protection laws.

  4. Ensuring agencies perform their duties effectively.

  5. Promoting honest advertisement and fair competition.

SUMMARY

Consumer rights and redress are essential parts of consumer protection. They ensure that consumers are treated fairly, receive safe products, and can get justice when wronged.

A responsible consumer should know his or her rights, make complaints politely, and report any unfair treatment to the appropriate authorities.

Providing redress benefits both the consumer and the business by promoting fairness, trust, and satisfaction in every transaction.
