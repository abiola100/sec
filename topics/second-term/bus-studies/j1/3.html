ETHICS IN SOURCING CHEMICALS


Ethics can be define as knowing what is right and wrong and making good choices. It helps us decide how to behave and treat others kindly and fairly.



ETHICS OF SOURCING CHEMICALS FROM LICENSED SUPPLIERS


  • Ensuring the chemicals are sourced from suppliers who are properly licensed and authorized to sell them.

  • Making sure the supplier follows safety regulations to protect people and the environment.

  • Verifying that the supplier provides clear information about the chemicals and their uses.

  • Choosing suppliers who operate ethically and responsibly, avoiding those who engage in illegal or harmful practices.

  • Ensuring that the chemicals meet required standards and are safe for their intended use.


GOOD HANDLING TECHNIQUES


  1. Always read labels and safety data sheets to understand the properties and hazards of the material.

  2. Use appropriate personal protective equipment such as gloves, goggles, and lab coats to prevent exposure.

  3. Store chemicals in suitable, labeled containers that are designed for their specific properties and hazards.

  4. Keep chemicals in designated storage areas, following temperature and environmental conditions as specified.

  5. Move and handle materials gently to avoid spills, breakage, or accidental reactions.

  6. Work in well-ventilated areas or use fume hoods to minimize inhalation of vapors or dust.


GOOD DISTRIBUTION TECHNIQUES


  1. Create a clear plan for how and when products will be delivered.

  2. Choose trustworthy and suitable vehicles or methods for delivery.

  3. Make sure products are packed securely to avoid damage.

  4. Monitor stock levels to prevent running out or having too much.

  5. Write down details of shipments and deliveries for easy tracking.

  6. Inspect products to ensure they are in good condition before sending them out.

  7. Plan delivery routes to save time and reduce costs.

  8. Provide necessary paperwork like invoices and shipping labels.


PROPER DISPOSAL TECHNIQUES


  1. Dispose of waste according to local laws and guidelines.

  2. Separate waste into categories like recyclables, hazardous waste, and general trash.

  3. Use designated bins and containers for different types of waste.

  4. Recycle materials like paper, plastic, glass, and metals to reduce landfill use.

  5. Dispose of hazardous materials (like chemicals or batteries) at specialized facilities.

  6. Compost food scraps and yard waste to create useful soil additives.

  7. Reduce the amount of waste produced by reusing items and buying in bulk.

  8. Teach people about proper disposal methods and the importance of waste management.
