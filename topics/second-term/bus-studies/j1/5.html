BUSINESS ORGANIZATION




What is Business organization ?

A business organization is a set of people who work to produce, buy, and sell goods and services in order to make money. It can be put together as corporations, partnerships, or single proprietorships, each with its own set of rules and procedure.


TYPES OF BUSINESS ORGANIZATION


SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP


  • Sole proprietorship : This is a business managed and controlled by an individual, with no legal difference between the owner and the business.

    • ADVANTAGES OF SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP


    1. It is easy to establish sole proprietorship business with minimal paperwork and regulations.

    2. The owner of the business has full Control over all business decisions.

    3. The owner of the business retains all the profits from the business.

    4. The Income is reported on the owner's personal tax return, simplifying tax filing.

    5. The sole proprietor can easily change business operations or direction.


      DISADVANTAGES OF SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP


    1. The owner of the business is responsible personally for all business debts and business obligations.

    2. To raise money to start may not be easy, most of the time they are being limited to personal savings and loans.

    3. The owner takes care of all aspects of the business, which can be an heavy responsibility.

    4. The lifespan of the business may be limited if the owner dies or decides to stop operating.

    5. The business usually relies solely on the knowledge of the owner and abilities.




    PARTNERSHIP


  • Partnership : can be define as a business owned by the two or more people who share gains, losses, and responsibilities.

      • ADVANTAGES OF PARTNERSHIP


    1. In partnership business partners share the workload, responsibilities, and decision-making together .

    2. Partners in partnership business bring diverse expertise and resources to the business.

    3. partnership business is easy to form, with fewer regulations than a corporation.

    4. Profits and losses of the business are passed through to partners, avoiding double taxation.

    5. More partners can mean more financial resources for the business which increases the capital of the business.


      DISADVANTAGES OF PARTNERSHIP


    1. The debts of the business are personal responsibilities of partners, including those incurred by other partners.

    2. The Disagreements among the partners may result to conflicts and this may cause harm to the business.

    3. The profits are shared among partners, which can lead to dissatisfaction.

    4. Making decision may be slower due to the need for consensus among partners.

    5. Limited Lifespan:Th lifespan of the partnership may be limited if a partner leaves, unless otherwise agreed upon.




    COOPERATION


  • cooperation : can be define as a separate legal entity owned by shareholders, where the business itself is responsible for its own debts and obligations.


      • ADVANTAGES OF COOPERATION


    1. Personal assets of shareholders are protected from business liabilities.

    2. Corporations can issue stocks and bonds to attract investors therefor it is easy to raise capital.

    3. In cooperation business the business will continues to exist even if ownership of the business changes.

    4. Being a corporation can promote the business credibility with customers and suppliers.

    5. Corporations is big enough and can offer employee with the benefits of things like health insurance and retirement plans.



      DISADVANTAGES OF COOPERATION


    1. It involve more paperwork, cost , and legal requirements to establish a corporation .

    2. Twice tax deduction as profits are taxed at the corporate level, and dividends are taxed again at the shareholder level.

    3. The influence of shareholders are limited over daily operations of the business, which are managed by the executives and the board .

    4. Corporations are usually faced with more regulations and reporting requirements than other business types in a country.

    5. To maintain a corporation it usually involve more expenses, such as legal fees and annual filings etc.




    LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY


  • Limited Liability Company : this can be define as a mixed structure that offers the limited liability of a business with the tax advantages and flexibility of a partnership.


      • ADVANTAGES OF LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY


    1. personal assets of the owners are protected from business debts and liabilities. the liability of the owner is limited .

    2. The owners of the business can manage the business or appoint managers, with fewer formalities than corporations.

    3. limited liability companies have the options to choose to be taxed as a sole proprietorship, partnership, or corporation.

    4. The profits of limited liability companies can be distributed in various ways, and not necessarily tied to ownership percentage.

    5. limited liability companies usually have lesser annual requirements and formalities compaired to corporations.


      DISADVANTAGES OF LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY


    1. L Some states use to require limited liability company to be dissolved after a certain number of years or when a member leaves.

    2. To Create an operating agreement can be complicated and may require legal assistance.

    3. limited liability company owners may have to pay self-employment taxes on their share of profits.

    4. limited liability company may have a hard time raising capital when compared to corporations, as they cannot issue stock.

    5. limited liability company laws are different by state, which can create confusion and additional administrative work if it is operating in more than one states.




    COOPERATIVES


  • Cooperative : can be define as a business owned and managed by a group of individuals for their common benefit, often with a focus on the community and shared goals. 


      • ADVANTAGES OF COOPERATIVE


    1. It is owned and controlled democratically by its members, who have equal voting rights.

    2. The Profits realized from the business are distributed among the members based on their participation or use of the cooperative.

    3. Cooperatives Often focused on the community needs and member benefits rather than profit. it is generally not profit oriented.

    4. Cooperative enable Members to benefit from reduced costs or improved services.

    5. Cooperatives can continue even if membership changes, and thereby maintain the long term existence.


      DISADVANTAGES OF COOPERATIVE


    1. The democratic decision-making can be challenging as the process can be slow and may lead to conflicts among members.

    2. The profits are shared among members, which may limit individual financial gain from profit distributed.

    3. Raising of capital can be difficult, as cooperatives typically rely on member contributions for funding.

    4. To manage a cooperative can be complex, as it requires a strong commitment from it members.

    5. Cooperative only focus on member needs and benefits and may limit it ability to expand or diversify.





    NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION


  • Nonprofit Organization :A business that operates to serve a public or social cause, with profits reinvested into the mission rather than distributed to


      • ADVANTAGES OF NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION


    1. Nonprofits organization does not pay income tax on profits related to their mission.

    2. Nonprofits organization most of the time enjoys a positive public image,which may lead to donations and grants.

    3. Nonprofits organization are elligible to apply for public and private grants to support their mission.

    4. Nonprofits organization usually operates with the primary aim of benefiting the public or a specific define cause.

      5. Nonprofits organization protects board members and staff from personal liability for the organization's debts.


      DISADVANTAGES OF NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION


    1. Nonprofit organization must stick to strict regulations and reporting requirements to maintain tax-exempt status.

    2. Any surplus funds must be reinvested back into the mission, not distributed to owners or shareholders, and this may lead to limited profit potential .

    3. Nonprofit organization often relies heavily on donations, Grants or any other external funding, which can be unpredictable.

    4. Board members of the organization and donors may influence decision-making,which limit the flexibility for the organization leaders.

    5. Nonprofit organization may face challenges in attracting and retaining skilled workers due to limited financial resources.
