LIVING THINGS AND NON LIVING THING



Living thing:

What is living thing?

A living thing, or organism, can be define as any entity that has life.

Examples
  1. Humans: People who can think and talk.

  2. Trees: Big plants with trunks and branches.

  3. Mushrooms: Fungi that grow from the ground.

  4. Bacteria: Tiny organisms that can live in our bodies.

PROPERTIES OF LIVING THING

The acronym "MR NIGER D" is a helpful way to remember the characteristics of living things. Here's what each letter stands for:

  1. M - Movement: Living things can move, either by changing location or moving parts of their body.

  2. R - Respiration: They can break down food to release energy.

  3. N - Nutrition: They need food to provide energy and materials for growth and repair.

  4. I - Irritability (Response to stimuli): They can respond to changes in their environment.

  5. G - Growth: Living things grow and develop over time.

  6. E - Excretion: They can remove waste products from their bodies.

  7. R - Reproduction: They can produce new individuals of the same species.

  8. D - Development: They undergo changes throughout their life cycle.

USES OF LIVING THINGS
  1. Food: Plants give us fruits, vegetables, and grains. Animals provide meat, milk, and eggs.

  2. Medicine: Plants and some animals give us medicines to treat illnesses.

  3. Learning: Scientists study living things to understand how life works.

  4. Pollination: Insects help plants reproduce by moving pollen.

  5. Environmental Balance: Plants make oxygen and absorb carbon dioxide. Animals help keep ecosystems balanced.



Non-Living thing:


What is Non-living thing?

a non-living thing can be define as an object or material that does not have life.

Examples:
  1. Rocks: Hard, natural objects found on the ground.

  2. Water: A liquid that we drink and use for many purposes.

  3. Air: The invisible gas we breathe.

  4. Table: A piece of furniture with a flat top and legs.

PROPERTIES OF NON-LIVING THING
  1. No Growth: They do not increase in size or change over time on their own.

  2. No Metabolism: They do not consume food or produce energy.

  3. No Reproduction: They cannot produce offspring or make more of themselves.

  4. No Response to Stimuli: They do not react to changes in their environment.

  5. No Movement: They cannot move on their own.

  6. No Cellular Structure: They are not made up of cells.

  7. No Adaptation: They do not adapt to their environment over time.


USES OF NON-LIVING THINGS
  1. Building: Wood, bricks, cement, and metals build homes and buildings.

  2. Energy: Fossil fuels and renewable sources power our homes and cars.

  3. Tools and Tech: Plastics, metals, and silicon make tools, computers, and phones.

  4. Clothing: Cotton, polyester, wool, and nylon make clothes and shoes.

  5. Infrastructure: Asphalt, concrete, and metals make roads, bridges, and railways.
