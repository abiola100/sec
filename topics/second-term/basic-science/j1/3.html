IMPORTANCE OF LIVING AND NON LIVING




IMPORTANCE OF LIVING THINGS


  1. Food Source: Plants and animals provide us with food that we need to survive and stay healthy.

  2. Medicine: Many medicines and treatments come from living organisms, helping us fight diseases and stay well.

  3. Environmental Balance: Living things like plants produce oxygen, absorb carbon dioxide, and maintain ecosystems, which support life on Earth.

  4. Research and Learning: Studying living things helps us understand biology, genetics, and the natural world, leading to scientific advancements.

  5. Economic Value: Living things support industries like agriculture, forestry, and biotechnology, which contribute to our economy and provide jobs.




IMPORTANCE OF NON-LIVING THINGS


  1. Infrastructure: Non-living materials like metals, concrete, and asphalt build roads, bridges, buildings, and other structures that support our daily lives and communities.

  2. Energy: Non-living resources like fossil fuels and renewable energy sources provide power for electricity, heating, transportation, and industrial processes.

  3. Tools and Technology: Non-living materials are used to create tools, machines, and devices that make our work easier, improve communication, and advance healthcare and other fields.

  4. Clothing and Shelter: Fabrics and materials from non-living sources are used to make clothes, shoes, and shelter, protecting us from the elements.

  5. Cultural and Aesthetic Value: Non-living things like art, architecture, and historical artifacts contribute to our cultural heritage and enrich our lives aesthetically.
