PHENOMINA USING KINETIC ENERGY

kinetic energy is a crucial component that drives motion and enables various processes to occur.




  1. When a car or bike is moving, it has kinetic energy. The faster it goes, the more kinetic energy it has. This energy is what causes the vehicle to keep moving.

  2. When you apply the brakes, the kinetic energy is converted into heat energy due to friction between the brake pads and the wheels, slowing the vehicle down.

  3. When a player kicks a ball, they transfer kinetic energy from their leg to the ball, causing it to move.

  4. When a bat hits a ball, the kinetic energy from the swinging bat is transferred to the ball, making it fly through the air.

  5. Wind turbines harness the kinetic energy of moving air (wind) to generate electricity. The wind's kinetic energy turns the turbine blades, which spin a generator to produce electricity.

  6. In a hydropower plant, the kinetic energy of flowing water is used to generate electricity. The moving water spins turbines, which in turn drive generators.

  7. As a roller coaster descends, its potential energy is converted into kinetic energy, causing it to speed up.

  8. When you throw a ball, the kinetic energy from your arm is transferred to the ball, propelling it through the air.

  9. Your body converts stored energy (from food) into kinetic energy to move.

  10. Pedaling a bike converts your muscle energy into kinetic energy, propelling the bike forward.
