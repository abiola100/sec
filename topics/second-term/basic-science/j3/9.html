MAGNETISM

Magnetism is a fundamental force of nature that manifests as the attraction or repulsion between objects with magnetic properties.






FUNDAMENTAL ASPECT OF MAGNETISM
  1. Magnetic Field: The area around a magnet where magnetic forces can be felt.

  2. Magnetic Poles: The ends of a magnet where the magnetic force is strongest; north and south poles attract each other, while like poles repel each other.

  3. Magnetic Materials: Materials that can be turned into magnets or attracted by magnets, like iron, nickel, and cobalt.

  4. Electromagnetism: When electric currents create magnetic fields, and magnetic fields can create electric currents.

  5. Magnetic Domains : Small areas inside a material where the magnetic directions of atoms are lined up the same way, making the material magnetic.
