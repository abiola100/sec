SOLID MINERAL

Solid minerals are naturally occurring substances that are solid and inorganic, with a definite chemical composition and crystalline structure.






TYPES OF SOLID MINERAL
  1. ron Ore: Used to produce iron and steel, essential for construction and manufacturing.

  2. Gold: Valued for its rarity and used in jewelry, electronics, and as a financial asset.

  3. Diamond: The hardest known natural material, used in jewelry and industrial cutting tools.

  4. Bauxite: The primary ore of aluminum, used in packaging, transportation, and construction.

  5. Copper: Used in electrical wiring, plumbing, and as an alloy in bronze and brass.

  6. Coal: A fossil fuel used for electricity generation and steel production.

  7. Limestone: Used in cement production, as a building material, and in agriculture.

  8. Lead: Used in batteries, radiation shielding, and as a component in some solders.

  9. Zinc: Used for galvanizing steel, in alloys such as brass, and in the production of batteries.

  10. Gypsum: Used to make plaster, wallboard, and as a soil conditioner.

  11. Phosphate: Used in fertilizers, animal feed supplements, and industrial products.

  12. Manganese : Used in steel production and as an alloying element in aluminum and copper.
