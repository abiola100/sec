SOUND ENERGY

Sound energy is the energy carried by sound waves, which are produced when a vibrating object causes pressure variations in a medium such as air, water, or solids.






CHARACTERISTICS OF SOUND ENERGY
  1. Production of Sound: Sound energy is created by vibrations of objects, causing the surrounding medium to vibrate and generate sound waves.

  2. Transmission of Sound: Sound waves propagate through a medium via compressions and rarefactions, traveling from a source to a receiver.

  3. Reflection of Sound: Sound waves bounce off surfaces, influencing acoustics by creating echoes and reverberation in a space.

  4. Hearing: The auditory system detects sound waves, converts them into electrical signals, and processes them in the brain to perceive and interpret sounds.

  5. Absorption of Sound: Materials absorb sound waves, converting sound energy into heat energy and reducing sound intensity in an environment.
