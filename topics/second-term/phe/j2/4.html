Table tennis is a fast-paced indoor racket sport played on a table, where two or four players hit a lightweight ball back and forth using small paddles.


History of tennis

It was Originated in England in the (late 1800s) as a parlor game,it was Originally called ‚ÄúPing Pong‚Äù, Became an Olympic sport in 1988.



Importance of Table Tennis


  • It mproves hand-eye coordination.

  • It Encourages alertness and agility.

  • It Builds social skills through competition.

  • It Promotes discipline and strategic thinking.

  • It is Easy to learn, suitable for all ages.

  • There is Low injury risk compared to many sports.



Facilities & Equipment


  1. Table (2.74m long √ó 1.525m wide).

  2. Net (15.25 cm high).

  3. Paddle/racket (wood with rubber).

  4. Ball (40mm, celluloid or plastic).

  5. Indoor hall with flat, well-lit surface.


Basic Skills in Table Tennis


  1. Grip (shakehand, penhold)

  2. Stance and footwork

  3. Forehand and backhand drive

  4. Serve and return

  5. Smash

  6. Push and block



Rules and Scoring in table tennis


  • Game played to 11 points

  • Must win by 2 points

  • Best of 3, 5, or 7 games

  • Serve alternates every 2 points

  • A ball must bounce once on each side during a serve



Officials in table tennis and Their Duties


  • Umpire: Enforces rules, keeps score.

  • Assistant Umpire: Helps in decisions, observes net serves.

  • Scorer: Updates match score.

  • Timekeeper (optional in tournaments).



Advantages of Playing Table Tennis


  • It Builds mental focus.

  • It Improves quick thinking.

  • It is Good for indoor recreation

  • It is Suitable for disabled/elderly players

  • It is Inexpensive to start

  • it Helps manage stress



Disadvantages of Playing Table Tennis


  • Requires steady surface.

  • It is Less aerobic than outdoor sports.

  • It is Not widely played in some rural areas.

  • It Injuries (e.g. wrist strain) if not played properly.

  • Expensive competition-grade gear.

  • It May become monotonous without variation.




Lawn Tennis

Lawn tennis is a racket sport played between two (singles) or four players (doubles) on a rectangular court, divided by a net.


Origin and Development


Originated from ‚ÄúReal Tennis‚Äù in France/England, Modern form developed in 1873 by Major Walter Wingfield, First Wimbledon Championship held in 1877.



Importance of Lawn Tennis


  • It Improves muscular strength and coordination.

  • It Teaches tactical thinking.

  • It Encourages fair play and patience.

  • It Builds discipline and self-control.

  • It Can be played as a lifelong sport.



Court and Equipment for Lawn Tennis


  • Court: 23.77m long √ó 8.23m (singles) or 10.97m (doubles).

  • Net: 0.91m high at center.

  • Rackets: Strung oval frames.

  • Tennis balls: Felt-covered, pressurized.

  • Proper tennis shoes and attire.



Basic Skills in Lawn tennis


  • Serve (flat, topspin, slice).

  • Forehand and backhand strokes.

  • Volley and overhead smash.

  • Footwork and court positioning.

  • Drop shot and lob.

  • Return of serve.



Rules and Scoring in Lawn Tennis


  • Points: 15 ‚Äì 30 ‚Äì 40 ‚Äì game.

  • Deuce: 40-40, must win two consecutive points.

  • Sets: Best of 3 or 5 sets.

  • Faults: If serve misses twice.

  • Let: When the serve hits the net and lands in.



Officials in Lawn Tennis


  • Chair umpire: Primary decision-maker.

  • Line judges: Call lines (in or out).

  • Ball persons: Retrieve and supply balls.

  • Referee: Supervises tournament.



Advantages of Lawn Tennis


  1. It Enhances agility and balance.

  2. It Encourages teamwork in doubles.

  3. It Improves flexibility and reaction time.

  4. It Builds sportsmanship.

  5. It Provides full-body workout.

  6. It Improves endurance.



Disadvantages of Lawn Tennis


  • Risk of injuries (elbow, knee, ankle)

  • Weather-dependent for outdoor play.

  • Can be expensive (rackets, court fees)

  • Requires large playing area

  • It Needs regular training

  • It is Intimidating for beginners
