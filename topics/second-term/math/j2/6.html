Steps to Solve a Linear Inequality
  1. Rewrite the inequality, simplifying both sides (expand brackets, combine like terms).

  2. Isolate the variable by moving other terms (using addition/subtraction).

  3. Divide or multiply by the coefficient of the variable.

  4. If dividing or multiplying by a negative, reverse the inequality sign.

  5. Write the solution (e.g. x > 4, or y â‰¤ âˆ’2).

  6. Graph the solution on a number line (open / closed circle, shading).

  7. Check with a value to see if it satisfies the original inequality.

Common Mistakes Students Make

  1. Forgetting to flip the inequality when multiplying or dividing by a negative.

  2. Treating inequalities exactly like equations without regard for direction.

  3. Misplacing open vs closed circle on the number line.

  4. Not checking with a sample value.

  5. Writing the solution incorrectly or incompletely.
